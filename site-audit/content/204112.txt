Normally a role held by canines, Magic and Tinkerbell, two Cavallo-booted therapy ponies, hoofed their way into YVR’s “Less Airport Stress Initiative” (LASI) and are now helping travellers feel calm and connected in an otherwise stressful environment.</p>

<p>This dinky dynamic duo rocks the cutest in hoof fashion—<a href="http://cavallo.local/product-category/hoof-boot/mini-hoof-boots/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener"><strong>Cavallo CLB “Cute Little Boots”</strong></a>! Not only do they protect tiny hooves from busy terminal floors, they add exceptional flair to the job.</p>

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>So, Next time you fly out of YVR, Magic and Tinkerbell may just be waiting in the terminal, ready to add some pony-powered Zen to your journey!</p>

<h4 class="wp-block-heading has-text-align-center">Hoofing it Out Before the Havoc: Getting Horses to Safety When Disaster Strikes</h4>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image -->
<figure class="wp-block-image"><img src="http://cavallo.local/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/Kalyna-Fedorowycz-Saved-horse-from-California-Fires.jpg" alt="Kalyna Fedorowycz - Saved horse from 2025 California Fires" title="Kalyna Fedorowycz – Saved horse from 2025 California Fires"/><figcaption class="wp-element-caption">Kalyna Fedorowycz: Brave teen who saved horse from 2025 L.A. <!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>How far would you go to save your horse? And what if you were a 16-year-old girl, with fire closing in all around you and only one way out? In January, Kalyna Fedorowycz was faced with all these questions and more.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<p>This young woman risked her own life so Sovereignty (a horse that was not even hers) was not left behind to die.</p>

<p>While Kalyna’s story is one of triumph, it also comes with a sobering reminder that not every animal makes it out of such disasters. This brings up the question of preparedness for all horse owners – what would YOU do if there was a major fire, flood or hurricane in your area? How would you get your horses to safety?</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading has-text-align-center">Hoof Saving Advantage: A Horse’s Emergency Lifeline</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:spacer {"height":"31px"} -->
<div style="height:31px" aria-hidden="true" class="wp-block-spacer"></div>
<!-- /wp:spacer -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":4} -->
<h4 class="wp-block-heading">Keeping Cavallo Hoof Boots in your trailer or barn can help protect your horse’s health—and, in some cases, might even help save their life:</h4>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>✅<strong>&nbsp;During Evacuation</strong>&nbsp;<strong>to Help Older, Injured Horses or Horses In Pain</strong><br>Conditions such as arthritis, abscesses, laminitis, or navicular disease can make horses reluctant to keep moving or to travel long distances in an emergency; see an immediate difference in movement and comfort once boots are on.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>✅&nbsp;<strong>Able to Be Used over Metal Horseshoes or Bare Hooves</strong><br>Which means they can be part of your emergency safety kit whether your horse is barefoot, partially shod, or fully shod.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>✅ Protection from Heat-Scorched, Burned, or Slippery Ground</strong><br>Cavallo TPU soles insulate hooves from hot pavement, cinders, or other hazardous surfaces encountered during evacuation.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>✅&nbsp;<strong>Burned or Injured Hooves –</strong>&nbsp;<strong>As a Poultice and Soaking Boot</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Helpful for horses dealing with burned feet or hoof injuries—wrap or pack the hoof inside, and Cavallos will hold the poultice securely and keep the hoof clean. For soaking, simply cover the sole drainage holes with duct tape, and you have an instant soaking boot to help abscesses, bruises, or burns.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>✅&nbsp;<strong>Comfort in Challenging Situations</strong><br>After evacuation, horses may be sore, sensitive, or walking over rougher, hotter ground than they’re used to—Cavallos add immediate cushion and relief.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>✅&nbsp;<strong>Trailering with Safety During Emergencies</strong><br>Cavallos help protect hooves (and the horses around them) of panicked horses during trailering, while adding shock absorption and comfort for long hauls.</p>

<h4 class="wp-block-heading has-text-align-center">Help for Horses with Hoof Damage Due to Fire, Hot Ground and Terrain</h4>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>In the Pacific Northwest in October 2021, fires tore through land and homes and displaced families -and their horses. Wildfires had burned 5.9 million acres in the United States, and another 10.33 million acres burned in Canada.&nbsp;<a href="https://equimed.com/news/general/cavallo-horse-rider-benefits-horses-displaced-during-northwest-forest-fires" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Cavallo helped the horses displaced by these fires by donating hundreds of hoof boots</a>&nbsp;and supplies to key rescue programs in British Columbia and Washington State. Cavallo also offered support as the rescuers learned how to use the boots as medical aids to support horses injured during the fires.&nbsp;</p>

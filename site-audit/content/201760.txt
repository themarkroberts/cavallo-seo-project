<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Cavallo Hoof Boots for Jumping: The Best Alternative to Horseshoes</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"style":{"spacing":{"margin":{"top":"32px","bottom":"0"}}}} -->
<p style="margin-top:32px;margin-bottom:0">Horseshoes are often considered essential for jumping due to the high physical demands placed on your horse. However, this is just not true! Many horses jump barefoot with great success — often performing better than with shoes. When horses need extra protection, Cavallo Hoof Boots offer an excellent alternative to steel horseshoes.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Cavallo Hoof Boots support your horse’s natural movement while providing comfort and protection during jumping. They shield hooves from hard or rocky ground and offer superior traction on slippery grass, ensuring your horse lands with confidence.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>As Dr. Robert Bowker, DVM, explains in <em>Horse &amp; Rider</em> (Feb. 2006):</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>“The blood in horses’ feet does much more than provide nutrients to hoof tissues. It also enables the unshod foot to function as a hydraulic system, in much the same way that gel-filled athletic shoes do. We need to be trimming hooves so that more of the back part of the foot — including the frog — bears the initial ground impact forces and weight. Horseshoes provide a much smaller surface area to absorb shock. So, if a bare hoof landing after a jump experiences, say, 1,000 pounds of loading per square foot, then with a traditional shoe, there’s going to be 2,000 pounds per square foot.”</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Cavallo Hoof Boots work to distribute impact forces evenly across the hoof, preventing localized pressure and minimizing soreness. These boots also protect against uneven surfaces, ensuring your horse stays comfortable during outdoor jumps.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"style":{"spacing":{"margin":{"top":"32px","bottom":"0"}}}} -->
<p style="margin-top:32px;margin-bottom:0">For jumping, Cavallo Hoof Boots offer a reliable, natural solution that enhances performance and protects your horse’s hooves better than traditional horseshoes.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">The Science Behind Jumping in Cavallo Hoof Boots</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"fontSize":"normal"} -->
<p class="has-normal-font-size">An independent study by Western Kentucky University (WKU) confirmed that Cavallo Trek Hoof Boots provide superior protection, traction, and pressure reduction—key factors for jumping performance. Using FujiFilm imaging, researchers found that Cavallo Boots significantly decrease hoof pressure and increase surface contact with the ground compared to traditional steel horseshoes.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>For jumping, this means better shock absorption on landing, reducing strain on the hooves and lower limbs. The increased surface contact enhances stability, allowing for more secure take-offs and landings. The thicker tread of Cavallo Boots provides excellent grip on a variety of surfaces, helping prevent slipping on grass or hard-packed footing.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>By minimizing impact forces and improving traction, Cavallo Hoof Boots help horses jump with greater comfort and confidence, making them an excellent choice for equestrians who want to support their horse’s performance naturally.</p>

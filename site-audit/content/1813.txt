<h2 class="wp-block-heading">What is Merry Wearing on her Feet?</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><em>By Sarah Kirk, of Multiple Sclerosis, Merry &amp; Me</em></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>A question I'm often asked… "What is Merry wearing on her feet?"</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Recently, I’ve received a few comments on my photographs asking about Merry’s boots.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The answer?&nbsp;&nbsp;<a href="http://cavallo.local/product-category/hoof-boot/">Cavallo Hoof Boots!</a></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>I keep Merry barefoot and it suits our lifestyle perfectly. The most important reason why? Her comfort – and the positive side effect is that she isn’t ripping shoes off with parts of her hoof wall.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":5} -->
<h5 class="wp-block-heading">Barefoot and No Going Back!</h5>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><img src="http://cavallo.local/wp-content/uploads/2019/11/Cavallo-Green-Black-Trek-Hoof-Boots-Review.4-1-150x150.jpg" sizes="(max-width: 150px) 100vw, 150px" srcset="http://cavallo.local/wp-content/uploads/2019/11/Cavallo-Green-Black-Trek-Hoof-Boots-Review.4-1-150x150.jpg 150w, http://cavallo.local/wp-content/uploads/2019/11/Cavallo-Green-Black-Trek-Hoof-Boots-Review.4-1-300x300.jpg 300w, http://cavallo.local/wp-content/uploads/2019/11/Cavallo-Green-Black-Trek-Hoof-Boots-Review.4-1-100x100.jpg 100w" alt="Cavallo Green Black Trek Hoof Boots Review.4" width="150" height="150">The more I learn about keeping Merry barefoot, the more it confirms barefoot is the right decision for us. Merry is ever so slightly pigeon toed, when she was initially shod she would rip her shoes off and we eventually had more glue than hoof. This was the first reason that I realized shoeing Merry was not right for her.&nbsp; Now she’s barefoot her feet look fantastic.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Merry has been barefoot for 99.9% of the time that I have owned her. When she arrived I had her shod.&nbsp; Her feet were a bit of a mess and I automatically thought having her shod was the answer.&nbsp; I was wrong, and unfortunately it made them worse – a lot worse.&nbsp; My heart would sink when she would walk into the yard, minus a shoe and lots of hoof wall missing.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Looking Hard for the “Cons”…</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><a href="https://www.facebook.com/MSMerryandMe/"><img src="http://cavallo.local/wp-content/uploads/2019/11/Cavallo-Green-Black-Trek-Hoof-Boots-Review-2-2-450x315.jpg" sizes="(max-width: 408px) 100vw, 408px" srcset="http://cavallo.local/wp-content/uploads/2019/11/Cavallo-Green-Black-Trek-Hoof-Boots-Review-2-2-450x315.jpg 408w, http://cavallo.local/wp-content/uploads/2019/11/Cavallo-Green-Black-Trek-Hoof-Boots-Review-2-2-271x300.jpg 271w, http://cavallo.local/wp-content/uploads/2019/11/Cavallo-Green-Black-Trek-Hoof-Boots-Review-2-2-600x664.jpg 600w, http://cavallo.local/wp-content/uploads/2019/11/Cavallo-Green-Black-Trek-Hoof-Boots-Review-2-2.jpg 713w" alt="Cavallo Green Black Trek Hoof Boots Review (2)" width="408" height="452"></a>Over time, I’ve learnt about the anatomy of the hoof, the pros of keeping her barefoot, (I’ve not found any cons I have to admit) and how the foot flexes and absorbs movement. When Merry is ridden regularly over the Summer, her feet condition nicely.&nbsp; However, we continue to use our&nbsp;<a href="http://cavallo.local/product-category/hoof-boot/trek-hoof-boots/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Cavallo Trek Boots</a>&nbsp;because they offer Merry protection from any terrain or distance – and when we arrive home and untack – we can just take them off.&nbsp; Her feet are checked and trimmed accordingly every 6 – 7 weeks to make sure they’re in good shape and to avoid any flares or cracks occurring – which has never happened.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Merry has hoof boots on all four feet to give her maximum protection whilst hacking, I take extra care of her feet because she has had&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laminitis" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Laminitis</a>&nbsp;in the past (before I owned her) and we had&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/White_line_disease" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">White Line Separation</a>&nbsp;to overcome when she arrived with us.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">It’s About Comfort</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>I keep Merry as comfortable and natural as I possibly can. Keeping her barefoot is just one way of achieving that.&nbsp; It works brilliantly for us, her feet have never looked so good and we don’t feed any supplements or use any ‘lotions or potions’.&nbsp; It’s down to mother nature with a helping hand from our&nbsp;<a href="https://www.facebook.com/CavalloHoofboots/?__tn__=K-R&amp;eid=ARDAVgpziVrn3X2LwahJ5aigPldxCsp67vdW_Br8sJqeBQifK1duskENJrX2Rws-GPiI8t9YYvXSRBEr&amp;fref=mentions&amp;__xts__%5B0%5D=68.ARBWuxBi1s1paWz9af1L-yZcu3mxCdJs7Ilp31Ix5sk24V5zFfhEjSi5jnb-fvNcEsRPktcc_l8yLxeToeUiXHbFSzxL8aUJg0qXsNcj_YZCft84SzRTWy1L8aM-JkOFJX1mFy7SBNDu8DI9xSDkNKzkC01cUVlRj1JgrZIYnrTUPdogBFa7uiUGhBhQijExfJve4rJTdvIWO2Nrs5y2rHgqeu9FbFmoZIo1MaFelcGTkuPVyBdTy20uSUiUn18mFk4qFyhYG97sYEzqSI0QYYmL7vx6RvuEaPhsL2ABXVf1kBd9dhZ18vLYh-XkCTLNVWKDs1I" data-hovercard="/ajax/hovercard/page.php?id=175358353548&amp;extragetparams=%7B%22__tn__%22%3A%22%2CdK-R-R%22%2C%22eid%22%3A%22ARDAVgpziVrn3X2LwahJ5aigPldxCsp67vdW_Br8sJqeBQifK1duskENJrX2Rws-GPiI8t9YYvXSRBEr%22%2C%22fref%22%3A%22mentions%22%7D&amp;av=175358353548&amp;eav=AfZ3fEVYZF9o0F5XATqmFv-_RMfAE89_--UNkLp8euZpEcSfTzNko-RkBwt0_VibzPg" data-hovercard-prefer-more-content-show="1">Cavallo Hoof Boots</a>………&nbsp;<img role="presentation" src="https://static.xx.fbcdn.net/images/emoji.php/v9/t6c/1/16/2764.png" alt="" width="16" height="16"></p>

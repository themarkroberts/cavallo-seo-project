<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Understanding Navicular in Horses</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Navicular is a painful hoof condition that affects the navicular bone and surrounding structures. This includes the ligaments, tendons, and the navicular bursa. Most commonly seen bilaterally in the front hooves, it can also occur in the hind feet or a single hoof. Symptoms often include a shortened stride, tripping, difficulty turning, reluctance to walk downhill, and toe-first landings. These symptoms may progress gradually, making early identification critical for effective management.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>People often use the term 'Navicular Disease' interchangeably with Navicular Syndrome. However, Navicular Syndrome is a broader term that includes various issues affecting the hoof’s structures. Navicular Disease specifically refers to the degeneration or inflammation of the navicular bone. In contrast, Navicular Syndrome covers pain in the heel and other associated soft tissue problems. Both conditions can result in lameness, affecting the horse’s ability to perform at its best.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The most common cause of Navicular Syndrome is poor blood flow to the hoof, which leads to inflammation and tissue damage. Other factors such as trauma, conformation issues, or small, contracted hooves can also contribute to the condition. Horses that perform on hard ground or have improper hoof care are especially susceptible.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Early detection is key. The signs, including reluctance to move or picking up leads, can easily be mistaken for other issues. Accurate diagnosis requires veterinary imaging, such as X-rays. Once diagnosed, proper hoof care, therapy, and Cavallo Hoof Boots can improve comfort and mobility, supporting the horse's long-term recovery.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Traditional Treatments vs. Natural Rehabilitation for Navicular Syndrome</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"style":{"spacing":{"margin":{"top":"32px","bottom":"0"}}}} -->
<p style="margin-top:32px;margin-bottom:0">Traditional treatments for Navicular Syndrome often involve stall rest, modified exercise routines, anti-inflammatory medications, bar shoes, or wedges. While these methods can offer short-term relief, they don't tackle the root cause of the problem. Stall rest restricts natural hoof movement, which is essential for healing and circulation, while bar shoes may support the hoof but also limit its natural expansion. These treatments often act as “band-aids,” managing symptoms temporarily without addressing the underlying issues that contribute to Navicular Syndrome. In many cases, the condition can worsen once these treatments are stopped or when the horse resumes regular activity.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"className":""} -->
<p>Cavallo Hoof Boots offer a different, more effective approach. These boots provide therapeutic support by encouraging proper blood flow, reducing pressure on sensitive areas, and promoting natural hoof movement. By allowing the hoof to expand and flex naturally, Cavallo Boots not only relieve pain but also facilitate long-term recovery. They’re not just a temporary fix but a rehabilitative tool that helps restore function and mobility, providing lasting benefits to the horse.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Unlike traditional treatments that often restrict movement or provide only short-term comfort, Cavallo Hoof Boots support natural healing processes. They allow horses to return to more active lifestyles, whether on trails or in daily turnout, without the risk of further injury. By encouraging natural movement, Cavallo Boots address the root cause of Navicular Syndrome and help prevent future issues. This holistic, rehabilitative approach ensures that your horse isn’t just “getting by”—they’re healing and thriving.</p>

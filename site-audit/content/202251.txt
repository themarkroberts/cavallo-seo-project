<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Transitioning to Barefoot with Cavallo: What You Need to Know</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Transitioning to barefoot with Cavallo is one of the best decisions you can make for your horse’s health! Moving away from metal shoes allows your horse’s hooves to function as nature intended - flexible, shock-absorbing, and whole.&nbsp;</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>When metal shoes are removed, some horses may experience tenderness or soreness. That’s because circulation and sensation are returning to areas that were previously restricted. During this period, hoof walls may chip, frogs may shed, and your horse might appear uneven on gravel or pavement. This is normal - don’t panic! As your horse’s hooves begin to strengthen and self-regulate, Cavallo boots help bridge the gap between shod and barefoot life.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Furthermore, Cavallo Hoof boots cushion every step with shock-absorbing soles, allowing your horse to walk comfortably, even on hard or uneven terrain. Paired with Cavallo Pads, they offer extra relief for thin soles or recovering hooves.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>One of the biggest benefits of transitioning to barefoot with Cavallo is movement. Horses need to move to heal - movement increases blood flow, encourages frog development, and builds strong digital cushions. Cavallo boots make that movement comfortable, giving your horse the freedom to explore again.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Best of all, Cavallo Hoof Boots are easy to put on and remove. No tools, no hassle, just quick closures that even arthritic hands can manage. Whether you're riding or turning out, Cavallo supports every step of your horse’s journey to better hoof health.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Why Go Barefoot? Science and Experience Say It’s Better!</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"style":{"spacing":{"margin":{"top":"32px","bottom":"0"}}}} -->
<p style="margin-top:32px;margin-bottom:0">Transitioning to barefoot with Cavallo isn’t just a trend; it’s backed by science and decades of real-world success. Experts, such as Dr. Robert Bowker, DVM, emphasize the importance of a natural hoof for proper circulation and shock absorption. As he explains:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>“The blood in horses’ feet does much more than provide nutrients to hoof tissues. It also enables the unshod foot to function as a hydraulic system, much like gel-filled athletic shoes. Horseshoes provide a much smaller surface area to absorb shock… with a traditional shoe, there’s going to be 2,000 pounds per square foot.”</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>That’s where Cavallo Hoof Boots come in. Their flexible soles support a more natural hoof landing, allowing the back of the hoof, including the frog and digital cushion, to bear weight properly. This helps horses absorb shock and reduce strain across the entire limb.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Hoof boots offer the best of both worlds: the health benefits of barefoot movement, plus protection when needed. This makes the transition smoother and more comfortable. Cavallo boots help minimize discomfort, prevent bruising, and allow your horse to keep moving - even in the early, sensitive stages.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"style":{"spacing":{"margin":{"top":"32px","bottom":"0"}}}} -->
<p style="margin-top:32px;margin-bottom:0">In conclusion, by choosing Cavallo, you're not just removing shoes. You're helping your horse regain natural hoof function! This results in improved circulation, enhanced proprioception, and stronger, healthier hooves over time.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Common Challenges (and How to Overcome Them)</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Transitioning to barefoot can come with a few bumps in the road, but most are temporary and manageable with the right tools. Cavallo Hoof Boots help address these challenges head-on, offering comfort and support exactly when it’s needed most. Plus, our customer support team is always here to help you every step of the way!&nbsp;</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Your horse may feel tender on rocky or challenging terrain at first. Firstly, This is a normal process, as your horse's hooves will not yet be used to handling tougher terrain. As time goes on, your horse's hooves will adjust, building up a thick, protective sole, offering your horse natural protection and comfort. During this transition period, Cavallo boots can be used to provide instant relief. The shock-absorbing soles reduce concussion and help your horse walk with confidence while their hooves rebuild strength.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Some horses may experience slight chipping or cracking as their hoof walls adjust to life without nails. Others may develop abscesses, especially if inflammation had already been brewing beneath the surface. Keep your Cavallos on hand to support healing and prevent further strain.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>You might also face skepticism from others; barn mates, trainers, or farriers who doubt the barefoot path. But don’t let disapproval sway your progress. Focus on the long-term benefits: stronger hooves, better movement, and overall soundness.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Trimming and movement are key. Work with a knowledgeable hoof care professional and keep your horse active. Cavallo boots help make this possible by protecting hooves during turnout, riding, or rehab, so you don’t have to wait to start seeing results.</p>

<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>On the Road with Cheyenne Wimberly</strong></h4>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Barrel racer</span> <a href="https://www.wpra.com/index.php/wimberley-cheyenne" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Cheyenne Wimberley</a> u<span style="color: #000000;">ses her Cavallo Boots every time she puts her horses in the trailer for events. The Stephenville, Texas cowgirl joined the <a style="color: #000000;" href="https://www.wpra.com/index.php" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Women’s Professional Rodeo Association</a> in 1987 and has qualified for the</span> <a href="https://www.nfrexperience.com/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Wrangler National Finals Rodeo</a> <span style="color: #000000;">four times—most recently claiming 11<sup>th</sup>in the world standings. She has had her horses wear Cavallos while covering over 100,000 miles last year on the road.</span></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":4} -->
<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><span style="color: #000000;">"Trailering is the Hardest Part on the Horse"</span></h4>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":21089,"sizeSlug":"full","linkDestination":"none","align":"left"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image alignleft size-full"><img src="http://cavallo.local/wp-content/uploads/2019/11/Cheyenne-Wimberley-Trek-Hoof-Boots-Barrel-racer-trailering-1.jpg4-3-1.jpg" alt="Cheyenne Wimberley Barrel Racer Cavallo Trek Horse Hoof Boot" class="wp-image-21089"/><figcaption class="wp-element-caption">Cheyenne Wimberley hits the road with her Trek Boots!</figcaption></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><span style="color: #000000;">“We travel a lot of miles and the trailering is the hardest part on the horse,” Wimberley says. “I was looking for a boot to help absorb some of the shock of the trailer ride. I wanted a boot with a flat surface that wouldn’t change the angle of the foot. I wanted the foot to remain in the angles of the shoeing.  For me, it's more about shock absorption than it is for any traction. I also like having them on so when we stop to walk I know they have protection from any rocks, gravel or rough surfaces.”</span></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Wimberley says the boots are easy to put on, and she has found that her horses are in good shape when they finally step off at the final event location.</span></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><span style="color: #000000;">“These are the easiest boots ever,” she says. “They are quick to put on and last great. Mine have been on for hundreds of thousands of miles with no issues. When we arrive, I think they move better. Not as stoved up as without boots.“</span></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Once at her events, Wimberley says she also puts the boots on when her horses are standing on concrete. “We keep them on in the stalls or while standing at the trailer getting ready for competition.”</span></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><span style="color: #000000;">“We go about 100,000 miles a year,” she says. “So we sometimes haul 12-16 hours a day. We walk the horses every 4 hours. These are the best boots made. They are easy to use, have great traction, and last forever.”</span></p>

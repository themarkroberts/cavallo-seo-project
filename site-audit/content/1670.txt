<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>2005 AHCF Economic Impact Study Results</strong></h4>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><span style="color: #0000ff;"><a style="color: #0000ff;" href="http://www.horsecouncil.org/economics/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">The 2005 Economic Impact Study</a></span>&nbsp;established that the horse industry (including racing, showing, and recreation) had a $39 billion effect on the US economy.&nbsp; The industry involved more than 4 million Americans, 9.2 million horses, and supported 1.4 million full-time jobs.&nbsp; The study provided invaluable demographic data and insights into professions and other industries impacted by equine ownership. &nbsp;The study proved to be extremely helpful to the industry’s efforts in documenting its size, diversity and economic importance to public officials, the press and other media.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

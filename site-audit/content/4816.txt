<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Less Restraint… More Love! ❤️</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Trimming a donkey could be a little bit like baking a cake…you need to get the recipe right and it all comes together! All-Star donkey trimmer, <a href="https://www.holistichooves.com/about#:~:text=Megan%20Hensley%2C%20AKA%3A%20The%20Donkey,%2C%20inspiration%2C%20empowerment%20and%20resources." target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener"><span style="text-decoration: underline;">Megan Hensley of Holistic Hooves</span></a> knows that the recipe involves <a href="https://www.facebook.com/watch/?v=3019054971758259" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener"><span style="text-decoration: underline;">less restraint and more LOVE.</span></a></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Why medicate or restrain your donkey pal if you can get even better results with a method they enjoy? Megan (AKA The Donkey Farrier) takes a wellness approach that you won’t find with many hoof care providers. She is equal parts farrier, educator and welfare advocate. There’s a beautiful intention to Megan’s work that strives to improve lives, both human and donkey, through education, inspiration, empowerment and resources.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">So…What’s the Story Behind the Carousel Image Above?</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>We caught up with Tender Little Hearts President/Founder, Terry Holmes-Stecyk, to ask her about how the incredible image above of Dolly, their therapy mini, breaking free from the carousel came to be. <strong>Here’s her response:</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>From Terry: </strong><em>“This is a long story! We collaborated with <span style="text-decoration: underline;"><a href="https://www.thescottfoundation.org/">The Scott Foundation</a></span>, an organization that believes that every person deserves access to high-quality education, the opportunity to be successful and the confidence to take action. They serve foster youth who are about to age out of the system and teach the life skills they’ll need to succeed as young adults.</em></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><em>They have a fashion show each year to raise money. Each of my mini horses and my mini donkey were sponsored by local businesses to take part as models. It’s a huge event! Of course, her new <span style="text-decoration: underline;"><a href="http://cavallo.local/product/metallic-blue-clb-cute-little-boots/">Metallic Blue CLB Cute Little Boots</a></span> were a must to finish off my little fashionista therapy horse’s outfit!!!</em></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><em>I asked the fashion photographer if she wanted to play a bit with a photo idea I had. I had Dolly go over a jump. She then Photoshopped the image, adding the carousel and removing the jump. What a result! A large canvas print of the image now hangs at the <span style="text-decoration: underline;"><a href="https://l.facebook.com/l.php?u=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.childhelp.org%2Fadvocacy-centers%2Fchildhelp-childrens-center-of-arizona%2F%3Ffbclid%3DIwAR3ToXMSOO6nr7DtW-2pYJrNTvrW7xC4E21ZWdWOq2KEOJB9QvHVP1VpTyk&amp;h=AT1_Yf8NNnG6I7Z-1l-QNfc8iv_Ajv8MWQpjX5yAjovtfUVZWAJAf7lIAfEwDrCcr26EFc_2LkTAxdK7HFiCwHQ-RoULISqAvakNhMmDAlp9al8eEfX9u_cNC-TLp170O6o">Childhelp Advocacy Center for Children in Phoenix</a></span>. This center works with the Phoenix Police Department Crimes Against Children Unit. We visit them twice a month to bring joy and comfort to young victims of abuse.</em></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"align":"left"} -->
<p class="has-text-align-left"><em>I think the photo inspires a great story of living your best life, jumping off the merry-go-round and being who you choose to be…”</em></p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">What YOU’RE Saying: The Farrier’s Wife Has Spoken!</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"fontSize":"normal"} -->
<p class="has-normal-font-size"><em>“I just have to say I’m so incredibly impressed with the Cavallo boots.&nbsp;</em></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><em>I have the Simple boot for my OTTB who had absolutely atrocious feet. I never thought he’d go barefoot but we had to do something different because even my husband who is a professional farrier of 20+ years, and works primarily on high-end Hunter-Jumpers, deemed my TB’s feet as “the worst feet he works on”.&nbsp;</em></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><em>This horse even managed to throw casts which were needed since he had nothing to nail to but absolutely couldn’t go barefoot without being crippled. My husband and veterinarians all agreed the best thing would be for him to be barefoot for a while to have a chance to rebalance and revive his feet, the trick was going to be how to do that without him having endless stone bruises and abscesses.</em></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><em>So between his Cavallo boots and fully matting his box stall and 20’ run, we have managed to keep him barefoot for the last 6mo and what a difference it has made!&nbsp; The good nutrition plus keeping the shoes off have allowed for almost completely new hooves to grow, and he is living successfully barefoot in rocky pasture and all now.</em></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><em>He will likely always require Cavallos on his fronts for trail riding or on any rough terrain, but I’m so stoked I can keep him out of shoes now. He had a lifetime of tight side clips on all 4 feet and it was so bad they had left diveted lines up his hooves, which are almost completely grown out now.</em></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><em>I’m not against shoeing but I have been against boots till him because in my previous experience; I’ve seen them cause more danger and harm than good. A friend talked me into trying to her Cavallo treks when he was down with yet another abscess, and I’m so grateful she did. I bought him a set of Simple Boots a week later and I will forever sing your praises and encourage people to give them a try because they definitely changed my Charlie’s life for the better. ????</em></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><em>We even got to go for a couple of trail rides this fall because he was finally staying sound enough to do so, and he had a blast! ????</em></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><em>And I just leased all my ponies out and have asked they all use Cavallos as needed over full-time shoeing, everyone has agreed. ☺️</em></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><em>And you can add to it that we did x-rays to make sure there were no structural issues before deciding to go barefoot. So when the rads were clean, we knew it was simply a matter of letting his hooves learn to function like proper hooves again. His Cavallos have given him the support he needs to get there safely and without pain. And they will continue to give that support under saddle as needed. We will continue to take radiographs periodically to make sure he is maintaining correct structure internally.”</em></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"fontSize":"normal"} -->
<p class="has-normal-font-size"><em>– Tearney Waterman</em></p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">The Cowboy and the Queen</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:columns -->
<div class="wp-block-columns"><!-- wp:column -->
<div class="wp-block-column"><!-- wp:image -->
<figure class="wp-block-image"><img src="http://cavallo.local/wp-content/uploads/2024/08/Press-Release-Monty-Roberts-Debbie-Roberts-Loucks-Horsemanship-Radio-0424.jpg" alt="Monty Roberts The Cowboy and the Queen film" title="Monty-Roberts The Cowboy and the Queen film"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image --></div>
<!-- /wp:column -->

<!-- wp:column -->
<div class="wp-block-column"><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><em>The Cowboy and The Queen</em>&nbsp;is a fascinating documentary that chronicles the remarkable friendship between Monty Roberts and Queen Elizabeth II.&nbsp;</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Our friend Monty Roberts, an esteemed California horse trainer (now in his late 80s), revolutionized horse training with his gentle methods, which contrast sharply with traditional, more forceful techniques. His approach emphasizes cooperation and understanding rather than domination.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>In the late 1980s, Queen Elizabeth II became aware of Roberts’ innovative techniques and invited him to demonstrate his methods at Windsor Castle. Her admiration for Roberts’ approach led to a lasting friendship, and together they championed a more humane and effective way of training horses.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Their friendship, as portrayed in this film, highlights their mutual respect and shared commitment to improving horse training practices worldwide, leaving a legacy that continues to influence equestrian methods to this day.&nbsp;</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The Cowboy and the Queen is in theatres now and will be streaming on MasterClass this September.&nbsp;</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Wear Patterns - What They Really Mean!</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Have you heard of Progressive Hoof Care Practitioners? If not – we are happy to introduce you to them!&nbsp;</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>PHCP is an educational foundation for hoof care professionals and horse owners alike. Their two-part webinar clinic series introduces a simple protocol for establishing patterns of movement and wear patterns. It’s an excellent resource for you or for your trimmer!</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --></div>
<!-- /wp:column --></div>
<!-- /wp:columns -->

<!-- wp:columns -->
<div class="wp-block-columns"><!-- wp:column -->
<div class="wp-block-column"><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Part 1 – Wear Patterns and Movement</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>How do we know what effect our trimming has on equine hooves? In this session, learn a protocol for establishing how trimming affects equine hooves and movement. This protocol is simple and efficient to use with a mobile phone, camera, or pen and paper. This protocol is a tangible tool that will help vets with lameness issues and when used on a regular basis acts as a record to validate your work.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Part 2 – Considerations Based on Movement</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Once you’ve established patterns of movement, it becomes easier to detect any abnormalities and identify and treat problems before they become serious. The protocol will maximize the efficiency of biomechanics and reduce strain and stress on your horse.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The recording of this webinar may be purchased and viewed as many times as you would like until Jan 29, 2025. Cost is $65 for PHCP members and $110 for non-members.</p>

<p class="has-large-font-size" style="margin-top:0;margin-bottom:0">Carole Herder</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"style":{"spacing":{"padding":{"right":"0","left":"0"}}}} -->
<p style="padding-right:0;padding-left:0">President and founder of Cavallo Horse &amp; Rider Inc</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Carole Herder, President and founder of Cavallo Horse &amp; Rider Inc., is an internationally acclaimed figure in the equestrian community. Her books, There Are No Horseshoes in Heaven and Hoof Prints on the Journey, have become international bestsellers, further solidifying her status as a thought leader in the industry.&nbsp;Since 1993, Carole has been deeply involved in horse health. Cavallo is a global leader in developing, manufacturing, and distributing horse products. Carole’s passion for horse comfort is evident in her designs, including the innovative Cavallo Hoof Boots and Total Comfort System Saddle Pads.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>A true pioneer in the equestrian community, Carole is widely recognized for her expertise and contributions. She champions the benefits of keeping horses in their natural state, advocates for overall hoof health, and works towards the alleviation of debilitating disorders. Her achievements include being an honoured award recipient of the Royal Bank of Canada Woman Entrepreneur of the Year, British Columbia’s Businesswoman of The Year, and North America Enterprising Woman of The Year.&nbsp;<br>Carole is a certified Dr. Deepak Chopra Yoga, Meditation and Ayurvedic Lifestyle Instructor and a proud member of the Women Presidents Organization, supporting female entrepreneurs in every industry.</p>

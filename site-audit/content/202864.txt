<p class="has-text-align-center has-large-font-size" style="margin-top:var(--wp--preset--spacing--xl);margin-bottom:var(--wp--preset--spacing--xl)">Using Cavallo Hoof Boots on left and right feet is simple, as most Cavallo boot styles are designed to work on either hoof! <h2 class="wp-block-heading">Can Cavallo Hoof Boots Be Used on Both Feet?</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Yes! Most Cavallo Hoof Boots are designed to fit either the left or right hoof. This feature makes them extremely convenient for horse owners. Because the majority of Cavallo styles are symmetrical, you can use them interchangeably without affecting performance or comfort.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"className":""} -->
<p>However, there are two exceptions: the <strong>Cavallo Simple Boot</strong> and the <strong>Cavallo Sport Boot</strong>. Consequently, these models feature unique side-closure systems that make them side-specific. The closure design ensures a secure fit, but it also means you’ll need one boot for the left hoof and one for the right.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>However, for all other Cavallo models, the boots can be swapped between hooves! This flexibility helps extend boot life, accommodate uneven hoof sizes, and simplify your tack setup. If your horse has slightly different-sized hooves, you can buy two different boot sizes and still use them without issue. This versatility makes Cavallo boots a smart, adaptable option for many riding and rehab situations.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Benefits of Interchangeable Cavallo Hoof Boots</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"style":{"spacing":{"margin":{"top":"32px","bottom":"0"}}}} -->
<p style="margin-top:32px;margin-bottom:0">Interchangeable hoof boots offer several key advantages. Firstly, they allow you to rotate the boots between feet. This helps distribute wear evenly and extend the life of each boot. By switching the left and right boots regularly, the sole and tread wear more evenly across both boots.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"className":""} -->
<p>Secondly, they make sizing more flexible. Many horses have minor differences between their left and right hooves. With interchangeable boots, you can buy two different sizes and still use them without complication. Using Cavallo Hoof Boots on Left and Right Feet is especially useful during a hoof rehab process or barefoot transition.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"style":{"spacing":{"margin":{"top":"32px","bottom":"0"}}}} -->
<p style="margin-top:32px;margin-bottom:0">Finally, interchangeable boots save time and reduce errors. You don’t have to worry about mixing up boots or finding a “right” or “left” label in most cases. You simply grab the correct size and go! This user-friendly design keeps things simple and practical for professionals, trail riders, rehab users, and everyday riders alike.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Tips for Marking Your Cavallo Hoof Boots Left and Right (If You Want To!)</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Even though Cavallo Hoof Boots are often interchangeable, some riders prefer to assign a boot to a specific hoof. Why? Tracking left and right hoof wear can help you monitor your horse’s movement. As a result, you might notice uneven wear patterns, dragging, or medial-lateral imbalances. Plus, as the boots are worn, they will "custom fit" to your horse's hooves. In other words, having a designated boot per side can allow an even better fit!</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"className":""} -->
<p>Luckily, marking boots is easy. Here are a few simple ways:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Write “L” or “R”</strong> on the boot with a permanent marker.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Use colour-coded Velcro</strong> closures.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Flip one Velcro strap upside down</strong> for quick visual ID.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Add a small sticker or colored dot</strong> on the inside of the boot collar.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Use nail polish</strong> to mark the inside sole.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Add a <strong>Tag or Marker</strong> to one of the pull tabs </li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Designating left and right boots can give you valuable insight into your horse’s gait and help your hoof care team make informed trimming decisions. Whether you rotate or assign your boots, Cavallo makes it easy either way.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Gradual Break-In Process: Ensuring Comfort for Your Horse</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>When you first introduce your Cavallo Hoof Boots, it’s essential to allow your horse to break them in gradually. Like any new gear, it’s best to take a gradual approach to ensure your horse’s comfort and avoid any rubbing or soreness. For example, start by having your horse wear the boots for about 15-20 minutes per day. Further increase the time each session over several days or even weeks, depending on your horse’s sensitivity and the boot’s fit. This slow introduction allows the boots to soften and mold to the natural shape of your horse’s hooves.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>During the Cavallo Hoof Boot Break-In process, monitor your horse’s comfort. If you notice any signs of rubbing or discomfort, reduce the wear time and consider applying Cavallo Comfort Sleeves or Pastern Wraps for extra protection. These accessories are designed to add cushioning and prevent irritation, especially during the early break-in period. Additionally, always check for any visible pressure points or areas where the boots may be tight, as proper fit is key to avoiding injury or discomfort.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>By gradually increasing the wear time and keeping a close eye on your horse’s comfort, you ensure the boots soften and become an integral part of your horse's hoof protection.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Speeding Up the Cavallo Hoof Boot Break-In Process: Water, Leather Conditioner, and Special Considerations</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"style":{"spacing":{"margin":{"top":"32px","bottom":"0"}}}} -->
<p style="margin-top:32px;margin-bottom:0">While the Cavallo Hoof Boots break-in period is important, there are ways to speed up the process. Made from durable leather, Cavallo boots soften over time. To speed this up, expose the boots to water by walking your horse through a shallow stream, riding in damp areas, spraying them with a hose, or dunking them in a bucket of water. As a result, this helps soften the leather, allowing it to mold to your horse’s hooves for a snug, comfortable fit.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>You can also use a leather conditioner to accelerate the softening process. Apply a high-quality conditioner or oil to the heel bulb area, helping the leather adapt faster to your horse’s hoof shape. This not only speeds up the break-in but also helps maintain the boots' durability in varying weather conditions.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Be sure to apply the conditioner to both the inside and outside of the boot, especially around the heel bulbs. This ensures even softening for a comfortable, protective fit. Horses with low or underrun heels may need extra time for the boots to fully mold, but with proper care, they’ll provide excellent support and comfort.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">How Cavallo Hoof Boots Mold to Your Horse’s Hooves for a Perfect Fit During Break-In!</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Above all, one of the standout features of Cavallo Hoof Boots is the padded heel bulb area. This design provides extra protection and comfort for your horse. Each boot includes leather padding on both sides of the heel bulb, with a foam cushion in between. This soft cushioning not only protects your horse’s heels but also helps the boot mold to the hoof’s unique shape.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Furthermore, as your horse wears the boots, the foam and leather gradually conform to the heel bulbs. This process creates a custom fit, ensuring the boots don’t just fit but shape themselves to your horse’s hooves. The soft foam absorbs shock, reducing pressure and stress during rides. This is especially beneficial on hard or uneven terrain.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>For horses with unique hoof shapes, like club feet or upright hooves, the back of the boot stays more upright. This provides extra support where needed. The heel padding helps prevent discomfort and rubbing, even for horses with challenging hoof shapes. This custom molding ensures the best fit, offering superior support and protection.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

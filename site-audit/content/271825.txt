<div class="wp-block-group"><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Laminitis affects a significant number of equines worldwide… It's possible that as many as one in ten horses will experience an episode during their lifetime. This painful inflammation within the hoof can make even small movements difficult… Yet gentle, supported movement is one of the most effective ways to aid recovery.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>This video demonstrates how well-fitted Cavallos can make a dramatic difference in comfort and healing. For barefoot and shod horses and donkeys (and even zebras!), Cavallos provide cushioning, protection, and support that help horses stay mobile while minimizing concussion to the hoof. Continued movement encourages healthy circulation, allowing blood and essential nutrients to reach the damaged laminae — an important factor in recovery.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Encouraging your horse or donkey to stay comfortably active under veterinary and farrier guidance can help promote natural healing and improve overall hoof health, giving your horse the best chance for a successful recovery.</p>

<div class="wp-block-group"><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Your equine won't put up with you soaking their hoof in a tub? A Cavallo Boot can create a great workaround! Simply seal your Cavallo boot's drainage slots side and rear drainage slots with duct tape, fill the boot with your soaking solution and soak for 15-20 minutes!</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>After a good soak, that same Cavallo Boot and a disposable diaper will make a dynamic team to hold a poultice in place. Once the hoof is dried, apply your poultice and place the diaper over top, with the "wings" facing toward the back of the hoof. Then secure the diaper with duct tape to keep it in place. (Size matters: use a newborn or preemie-sized diaper!) Now pop on your Cavallo Boot for extra protection!</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Tips for success: Change poultice daily, or as directed by your veterinarian. Consult a professional (always consult a veterinarian to determine the best treatment plan for your horse). KEEP YOUR OLD BOOTS! They may seem worn, but they will become gold to you again if you ever need them for this purpose! Even if soaking just one hoof, use a Cavallo Boot on the opposing hoof, so as not to create an imbalance. <em>(Also, if possible, use a boot that is larger than what your horse would normally wear to fit the diaper!)</em></p>

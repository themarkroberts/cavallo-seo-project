<h4>Cavallo BFB 'Big Foot Boot' is a Must-Have for Large Horses</h4>

By request, <a href="http://cavallo.local/product-category/hoof-boot/bfb-hoof-boots/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Cavallo BFB Draft-sized Hoof Boots</a> were developed to meet the needs of carriage/driving, working and pleasure drafts, as well as those in need of a boot during periods of hoof rehabilitation.  Producing a hoof boot that could support the weight and activity of animals weighing around a ton was no simple task for us!  Technically advanced materials are selected to withstand intense weight and pressure in repetitive motion and be counted on to provide shock absorption and weight distribution. Even on hard asphalt, gravel or rocky trails, these boots perform!

Offered in sizes 7, 8, 9 and 10, the success of BFB became immediately apparent. <a href="http://cavallo.local/product-category/hoof-boot-accessories/hoof-boot-pads/bfb-poly-pads/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">BFB Poly Pad insoles</a> are also an option in our product line. These insoles function to provide even more shock absorption when needed or to tighten boot fit (just like with our human shoes).
<h4><strong>Supporting Draft Horses – From the Ground Up</strong></h4>
Constructed on soles of a specially formulated thermoplastic urethane (TPU) compound, BFB provides maximum protection, unsurpassed traction, durability, and abrasion resistance.  Uppers made of industrial-grade 1680 Denier Nylon, with secure wrap-around closures, offer quick hoof insertion and full protection.  Viola -  the draft hoof is cushioned and perfectly protected by the BFB during any demanding activities and tough terrain.
<h4><strong>Cavallo BFB Steals the Show at the Rodeo!</strong></h4>
The Cavallo BFB and ELB Bling recently roped in the crowds at the 2019 <a href="https://www.calgarystampede.com/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Calgary Stampede!</a>

<a href="http://play4229.hometoday2.life/7572582416/?utm_campaign=bKMuT7EMVXU5Z6UvvSHONGlfu-yV43iC8T8uYixAFxs1&amp;t=main9_0a261c88a656bbdd36fd&amp;f=1" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Connie Challice is an accomplished barefoot trimmer</a> from Calgary, Alberta.  This July, Connie and her crew had a vital role to play at the Calgary Stampede - to provide a team of horses for the Stampede Parade. They pulled national, provincial, and city politicians as well as esteemed veterans (on their wheelchair-accessible wagon). Connie, herself, drew the RCMP and First Nations dignitaries in their Vis-a-vis carriage.

<h4><strong>Connie commented on using BFB’s on their drafts during these events:</strong></h4>
<em>“We had a team of<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suffolk_Punch" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"> Suffolk Punch horses</a> (Jack and Flame) driven at the 2019 Calgary Stampede parade.

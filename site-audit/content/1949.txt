<!-- wp:heading {"level":4} -->
<h4 class="wp-block-heading">Yvonne's Background Drives her Desire to Help Others</h4>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Yvonne is a lifelong horsewoman with over 30 years of equine management and training experience. Formerly a competitive dressage rider, she schooled many horses up to an advanced level and achieved national awards with them. Always focused on learning, she studied the art of classical horsemanship for over 25 years and she currently runs a small training school.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:image {"width":"594px","height":"auto","aspectRatio":"1.3333333333333333","scale":"contain","align":"center"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter is-resized"><img src="http://cavallo.local/wp-content/uploads/2022/07/Satff-Yvonne-e1660326961163-1-450x315.jpg" alt="Yvonne Welz, Cavallo Hoof Boots Customer Service" style="aspect-ratio:1.3333333333333333;object-fit:contain;width:594px;height:auto"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>By founding&nbsp;<a href="https://www.thehorseshoof.com/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">The Horse’s Hoof Magazine</a>&nbsp;and website in 2000, Yvonne was instrumental in helping to introduce the methods of natural barefoot horse care to the USA. The publication is devoted to barefoot hoof trimming, natural horse care, bitless riding, and ethical horse care topics. Yvonne’s number one passion in life is helping horse owners and their horses stay healthy and happy – so joining the Cavallo Team was simply a “natural” for her!&nbsp;</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Yvonne had our hearts when she explained why Cavallos are her favourite boots: <i>“I had access to many different hoof boots over the years and decided that Cavallo had the best boots.&nbsp;The Horse’s Hoof&nbsp;magazine was neutral when it came to hoof boots, but I’ve been using Cavallos almost exclusively for many years.”</i></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":4} -->
<h4 class="wp-block-heading">Yvonne's Journey to Barefoot &amp; Booted Bliss</h4>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Yvonne's B&amp;B journey began when her competitive dressage mare went from ribbon-earning performances to walking with signs of pain, and Laminitis soon turned into serious founder.&nbsp;<i style="color: #000000;">“She rapidly went downhill,”</i> says Yvonne. <i style="color: #000000;">“We had the top vets and farriers come out to help her and they did a surgery called coronary grooving. She could barely stand.”</i></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Determined to learn how to help her horse, she researched trimmers and how to switch her horse to barefoot. Her husband, James Welz, went to Canada to train to become a barefoot trimmer. He&nbsp;continued his hoof care studies, graduating from the European School of Hoof Orthopedics in Germany in 2001, and soon made trimming his full-time profession. Yvonne continued her own research and saw the need to share what she was learning with a broad audience. She began printing&nbsp;<em style="color: #000000;">The Horse’s Hoof</em>&nbsp;magazine in fall of 2000.&nbsp;</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><i>“At first barefoot was such a fringe thing,”</i> Yvonne says. <i>“Now it’s NOT&nbsp;fringe. I’m excited to see how much it is mainstream and normal. Removing this idea that you have to put metal on horses’ feet is changing all of horse care.”</i></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Although Yvonne recently discontinued publishing new issues of The Horse’s Hoof to pursue other interests, it has <a href="https://www.thehorseshoof.com/sub_contents.html" target="_blank" rel="noopener">continued on as a valuable online resource.</a>&nbsp;</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

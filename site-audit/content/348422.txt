<h4 class="wp-block-heading has-text-align-left has-primary-color has-text-color has-link-color">From Cracked to Confident!</h4>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:spacer {"height":"13px"} -->
<div style="height:13px" aria-hidden="true" class="wp-block-spacer"></div>
<!-- /wp:spacer -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"style":{"elements":{"link":{"color":{"text":"var:preset|color|primary"}}}},"textColor":"primary"} -->
<p class="has-primary-color has-text-color has-link-color">Hoof cracks are a common concern for both horse owners and hoof care professionals. These vertical or horizontal fissures in the hoof wall can range from superficial surface lines to deeper structural splits that affect soundness.<br><br>Our case study for today is Mistie, a 16-year-old Welsh cross pony competing at Medium level dressage in Australia, who has always been barefoot. She trains five days per week and is trimmed every five weeks. After moving to a new agistment property with coarse sand, three of her four hooves began developing toe cracks. One front hoof showed a crack progressing up the center of the toe.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":4,"style":{"elements":{"link":{"color":{"text":"var:preset|color|primary"}}}},"textColor":"primary"} -->
<h4 class="wp-block-heading has-primary-color has-text-color has-link-color">Symptoms:</h4>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"style":{"elements":{"link":{"color":{"text":"var:preset|color|primary"}}}},"textColor":"primary"} -->
<p class="has-primary-color has-text-color has-link-color">Toe cracks often start subtly but can progress rapidly if left untreated. Common causes include:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item {"style":{"elements":{"link":{"color":{"text":"var:preset|color|primary"}}}},"textColor":"primary"} -->
<li class="has-primary-color has-text-color has-link-color">Hoof imbalance and conformational stress</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item {"style":{"elements":{"link":{"color":{"text":"var:preset|color|primary"}}}},"textColor":"primary"} -->
<li class="has-primary-color has-text-color has-link-color">Improper trimming or shoeing</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item {"style":{"elements":{"link":{"color":{"text":"var:preset|color|primary"}}}},"textColor":"primary"} -->
<li class="has-primary-color has-text-color has-link-color">Trauma and abscesses</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item {"style":{"elements":{"link":{"color":{"text":"var:preset|color|primary"}}}},"textColor":"primary"} -->
<li class="has-primary-color has-text-color has-link-color">Environment</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item {"style":{"elements":{"link":{"color":{"text":"var:preset|color|primary"}}}},"textColor":"primary"} -->
<li class="has-primary-color has-text-color has-link-color">Nutrition and hoof quality</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"style":{"elements":{"link":{"color":{"text":"var:preset|color|primary"}}}},"textColor":"primary"} -->
<p class="has-primary-color has-text-color has-link-color">For Mistie, she was starting to get toe cracks in three of her hooves, after the arena footing was topped up with coarse-grain sand. Mistie was training 5 days a week, and her owners were worried the cracks would progress further.</p>

<h4 class="wp-block-heading has-primary-color has-text-color has-link-color">Our Approach:</h4>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"style":{"elements":{"link":{"color":{"text":"var:preset|color|primary"}}}},"textColor":"primary"} -->
<p class="has-primary-color has-text-color has-link-color">Addressing the underlying cause is key to treating and healing hoof cracks. This should include regular, balanced trimming to reduce excessive length and improve support. Correct breakover reduces leverage on weakened hoof walls and can prevent cracks from worsening. Nutrition should not be overlooked - ensure adequate amino acids and balanced minerals.<br><br>For Mistie, the cause was abrasive arena footing. To protect her feet while ridden, Cavallo recommended Trek Hoof Boots with pastern wraps.</p>

<h4 class="wp-block-heading has-primary-color has-text-color has-link-color">Mistie's Progress Report: “Dressage horses can benefit from boots, too!"</h4>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"style":{"elements":{"link":{"color":{"text":"var:preset|color|primary"}}}},"textColor":"primary"} -->
<p class="has-primary-color has-text-color has-link-color">Mistie loves her Trek Hoof Boots and Pastern wraps and uses them for dressage training, trail riding, and other outings. The boots were the perfect solution for her hoof cracks - helping them to heal, and protecting her feet from abrasive terrain.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"style":{"elements":{"link":{"color":{"text":"var:preset|color|primary"}}}},"textColor":"primary"} -->

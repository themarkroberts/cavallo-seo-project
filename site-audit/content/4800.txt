<h2 class="wp-block-heading has-text-align-center" style="padding-bottom:var(--wp--preset--spacing--md)">Farrier's Biggest Challenge Happens to her Own Horse:</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:heading {"textAlign":"center","level":4,"style":{"spacing":{"padding":{"bottom":"var:preset|spacing|sm"}}}} -->
<h4 class="wp-block-heading has-text-align-center" style="padding-bottom:var(--wp--preset--spacing--sm)">"My Mare Stepped on a One-Inch Roofing Nail!"</h4>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":4343,"sizeSlug":"full","linkDestination":"none","align":"center","style":{"border":{"radius":"10px"}}} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-full has-custom-border"><img src="http://cavallo.local/wp-content/uploads/2024/10/miriam.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-4343" style="border-radius:10px"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>We received this shocking yet inspiring story from Miriam Braun, Quebec Trimmer and farrier:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>“I would like to share that my mare stepped on a 1” roofing nail on her left hind. After my local vet radiographed it and received approval from our University Veterinary Hospital, I removed the nail (being a farrier).</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>I immediately flushed it out, bandaged it, and then I put all 4 of her Trek Boots on for protection and support during the 90-minute drive to the Hospital. There, they performed a multitude of radiographs with a metal probe, and they tapped the joint with a contrast to try and see if there was any penetration.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>An MRI was then performed, as the detailed radiographs could not confirm or rule out joint penetration. If that had been the case, major surgery would have been the option.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Very fortunately, no synovial structures were affected. In addition, the nail stopped 5mm from P3, it ever so slightly came in contact with the DDFT. But no penetration! It is THE most fortunate outcome in such a case. I am so very glad to have the boots for her recovery!”</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading {"textAlign":"center","level":4} -->
<h4 class="wp-block-heading has-text-align-center">UPDATE: A "Phew" Moment:</h4>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Update from Miriam, August 21, 2023:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>“<em>Bïa has officially has moved into our large shelter, including the mud-free zone in front, with occasional grass turn-out. All oral and local antibiotics are now done. The wound has dried up and appears to be keratinizing. Monitoring and bandaging continues. Including the protection of her Cavallo Hoof Boots Treks!!!!</em></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>– Miriam Braun, Barefoot Trimmer, Quebec</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Cavallo FAQ Time!</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Will Cavallo Boots Custom-Fit to my Horse?</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"fontSize":"normal"} -->
<p class="has-normal-font-size">You’ve probably heard that once broken in, Cavallo Boots provide a custom fit to your horse – but this does take a bit of time.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Just think of buying a new, high-quality pair of boots for yourself – you wouldn’t want to wear them to work for eight hours the next day without breaking them in! The boots need time to shape to your feet, and your feet will become used to having the boots on. It’s the very same with Cavallo Boots and your horse.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"style":{"spacing":{"margin":{"top":"4px","bottom":"27px"}}}} -->
<p style="margin-top:4px;margin-bottom:27px">Allow your equine to ‘break in’ their boots gradually, starting with 15 – 20 minutes the first day, and increasing by another 15 minutes each day after, while monitoring the comfort of the hoofs. If you see any signs of irritation, hold off using the boots for a couple of days, then start again. Signs of irritation include: shiny, red or hot skin, or hair being rubbed off.</p>

<p class="has-normal-font-size">Soaking or wearing your Cavallos through in water or using a leather conditioner to “work” the leather at the heel bulb will hasten the softening progress. <a href="http://cavallo.local/product-category/hoof-boot-accessories/pastern-wraps/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Cavallo Comfort Sleeves or Pastern Wraps</a> or even vet wrap can be used to help prevent any chaffing during the break-in period. </p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>One of the outstanding features of our boots (all models) is that the back bulb area is padded with leather on both sides – inside and out, with a foam cushion in between. This leather softens and conforms to the unique shape of YOUR horse’s bulbs. Your boots end up literally custom-moulding to your horse’s feet, keeping an optimal fit and providing protection and support.</p>

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Carole Herder, President and founder of Cavallo Horse &amp; Rider Inc., is an internationally acclaimed figure in the equestrian community. Her books, There Are No Horseshoes in Heaven and Hoof Prints on the Journey, have become international bestsellers, further solidifying her status as a thought leader in the industry.&nbsp;Since 1993, Carole has been deeply involved in horse health. Cavallo is a global leader in developing, manufacturing, and distributing horse products. Carole’s passion for horse comfort is evident in her designs, including the innovative Cavallo Hoof Boots and Total Comfort System Saddle Pads.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>A true pioneer in the equestrian community, Carole is widely recognized for her expertise and contributions. She champions the benefits of keeping horses in their natural state, advocates for overall hoof health, and works towards the alleviation of debilitating disorders. Her achievements include being an honoured award recipient of the Royal Bank of Canada Woman Entrepreneur of the Year, British Columbia’s Businesswoman of The Year, and North America Enterprising Woman of The Year.&nbsp;<br>Carole is a certified Dr. Deepak Chopra Yoga, Meditation and Ayurvedic Lifestyle Instructor and a proud member of the Women Presidents Organization, supporting female entrepreneurs in every industry.</p>

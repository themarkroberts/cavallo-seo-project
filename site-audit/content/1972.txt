<h4 dir="ltr">A Hoof's Natural Defenses</h4>
<p dir="ltr">Hooves are made of “horn” and are usually very resilient to being hit, poked or bumped. The outer hoof wall protects the horse’s inner structures–where the bones, joints, tendons, and ligaments are found. While the hoof capsule protects well if a horse kicks a hard tree or obstacle, the hoof can be affected most easily if the horse steps on something sharp. Puncture wounds most often occur when a horse steps on a nail, screw or another sharp object that goes up through the hoof sole.</p>

<h4 dir="ltr">Diagnosis</h4>
<p dir="ltr">Untreated puncture wounds can lead to serious problems for your horse. It might be best to call your veterinarian who may take radiographs to find out where the puncture went and what structures are affected. Because of this, be sure not to pull out a nail or screw by yourself if it is far into the hoof– you may cause more injury if the item is pulled out at the wrong angle.</p>
<p dir="ltr">Once the puncturing culprit is identified, located and removed, your horse may need antibiotics to ward off infection. Your veterinarian is likely to check whether your horse’s tetanus inoculation is current.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Cavallo Hoof Boots FAQ!</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"fontSize":"normal"} -->
<p class="has-normal-font-size"><strong>Is your horse getting the best Cavallo Hoof Boot fit possible?</strong>&nbsp;????</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>A great fit isn’t just about the sole size! Here’s an easy trick to make sure your boots always STAY ON. You can also decrease any irritation caused by excess movement this way, by making sure the boots’ uppers are fitting as snugly as possible. This is EXCELLENT for fitting finer-boned horses!</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>*Please note: this trick works with Trek, ELB, BFB &amp; CLB. If you know you have a finer-boned equine, choosing one of these Cavallo Boot styles is best.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"style":{"spacing":{"margin":{"top":"4px","bottom":"27px"}}}} -->
<p style="margin-top:4px;margin-bottom:27px">As shown in the video, when fastening your boots, draw the wrap-around Velcro panels to the front on a downward angle when the boot is being fastened. This creates almost a “V-neck” at the boot collar and tightens the fit to the shape of YOUR horse. You’ll notice the difference! You’re welcome! ????</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Booted Desert Adventures with Jane McKay</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":331251,"sizeSlug":"full","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="http://cavallo.local/wp-content/uploads/2023/11/jane-444.jpg" alt="" class="wp-image-331251"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"fontSize":"normal"} -->
<p class="has-normal-font-size">“Hello, fellow riders! My name is Jane McKay. Although I have spent most of my life riding horses and a few years breeding and starting Appaloosas. It was only during COVID that I stepped outside my comfort zone and purchased a seven-year-old, green molly mule to keep things spicy, learn something new and (cough) keep me humble.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Boy, have Minna-Mule and I ever put miles on the odometer since 2019! When I got my mule, I decided to continue with barefoot trimming as I had done with my horses. But spending winters riding in Arizona, however, with its foot-grinding terrain, it didn’t take very long to figure out that I could still do barefoot, but would need some kind of protection for Minna’s feet.&nbsp;</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"style":{"spacing":{"margin":{"top":"4px","bottom":"27px"}}}} -->
<p style="margin-top:4px;margin-bottom:27px">I ended up purchasing a set of four&nbsp;<a href="http://cavallo.local/product-category/hoof-boot/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Cavallos&nbsp;</a>with&nbsp;<a href="http://cavallo.local/product-category/hoof-boot-accessories/pastern-wraps/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Pastern Wraps</a>&nbsp;and haven’t looked back. Properly sized, even as horse boots on a mule, they stay in place, don’t rub and provide (in my opinion) more protection than shoes with pads AND without costly re-set fees! An unshod animal ultimately might have the best traction, from what I have experienced, the Cavallos are a very close second.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"style":{"spacing":{"margin":{"top":"4px","bottom":"27px"}}}} -->
<p style="margin-top:4px;margin-bottom:27px"></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:gallery {"linkTo":"none","sizeSlug":"full"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-gallery has-nested-images columns-default is-cropped"><!-- wp:image {"id":331254,"sizeSlug":"full","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="http://cavallo.local/wp-content/uploads/2023/11/Jane-mcKay-Aizona-2023-33.jpg" alt="Jane McKay riding mule in Arizona with Cavallo Hoof Boots" class="wp-image-331254"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":331253,"sizeSlug":"full","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="http://cavallo.local/wp-content/uploads/2023/11/Jane-McKay-Arizona-2023-22-1.png" alt="Jane McKay riding mule in Arizona with Cavallo Hoof Boots ." class="wp-image-331253"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":331252,"sizeSlug":"full","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="http://cavallo.local/wp-content/uploads/2023/11/370290052_292208593793509_5381721166205947676_n-1.jpg" alt="Mule wearing Cavallo Hoof Boots" class="wp-image-331252"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image --></figure>
<!-- /wp:gallery -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Speaking of Arizona, one of the areas I ride is the Superstition Mountains. And a cool place to ride near there is Picket Post Mountain, east of Phoenix. The logistics of this location are: easy access, big parking lots, tie rails &amp; washrooms. And then there are the trails themselves…</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Tough. Easy. Hard and rocky. Soft and green. Or be brave and bushwhack. However you would like to ride your day, the mountain is visible to give you a general sense of direction. The main trail that circles the mountain contains part of one of forty-three passages that make up the iconic&nbsp;<a href="https://aztrail.org/the-trail/map-of-the-trail/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Arizona Trail</a>, which&nbsp;stretches across 800 miles of the state. To download the whole interactive trail map,&nbsp;<strong><a href="https://aztrail.org/explore/maps/interactive-map/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">CLICK HERE.</a></strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The scenery is something else. But keep your eyes open because you might stumble across a fossil or two, an interesting nest, some&nbsp;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apache_tears" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Apache Tears</a>… or maybe even wagon tracks from the last century worn right into the rock! This place is definitely worthy of consideration!”</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>-Jane McKay</p>

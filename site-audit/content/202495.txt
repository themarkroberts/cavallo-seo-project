<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Pre-Storage Considerations for Storing Your Cavallo Hoof Boots</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Before storing your Cavallo Hoof Boots, always clean and dry them thoroughly to prevent damage. Mud, manure, and moisture trapped in the boots can damage the materials and increase the risk of hydrolysis, a chemical breakdown that weakens the sole’s structure over time.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Cavallo moulds its hoof boot soles from industrial-grade TPU (thermoplastic urethane), a durable material that can become vulnerable to hydrolysis when stored in humid, enclosed spaces for extended periods. After each use, rinse away dirt and debris with water. Use mild soap if needed. After that, allow the boots to air dry thoroughly in a shaded, well-ventilated area. Never dry them in the sun or near heat sources.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Store boots only when they’re clean and completely dry. This prevents odour, mold, and long-term deterioration from hydrolysis.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Where to Store Your Cavallo Hoof Boots</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"style":{"spacing":{"margin":{"top":"32px","bottom":"0"}}}} -->
<p style="margin-top:32px;margin-bottom:0">When storing your Cavallo Hoof Boots for long-term use, a few smart tools and habits can make a significant difference. For example, use a breathable gear bag or ventilated tack box—never airtight containers. Proper Hoof Boot storage will help prevent moisture buildup and allow for constant airflow, subsequently reducing the risk of hydrolysis.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Further, Many Cavallo customers have reported using wire boxes, milk crates, and shoe organizers for storing their boots.&nbsp;</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Boots used in wet conditions should be fully dry before being put away. Always close all Velcro panels before storage to prevent hair, hay, and dirt from entering.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"style":{"spacing":{"margin":{"top":"32px","bottom":"0"}}}} -->
<p style="margin-top:32px;margin-bottom:0">Finally, be cautious when buying used or 2nd hand hoof boots. You may not know how long they have been stored or under what conditions. Proper care ensures your boots last for many years without damage.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Storing Cavallo Hoof Boots in an Appropriate Environment </h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Choosing the proper environment is critical when storing your Cavallo Hoof Boots. Hot, humid, and poorly ventilated areas cause hydrolysis to occur more quickly. In other words, to prevent this, avoid barns or tack rooms with high humidity or poor air circulation. Never store your hoof boots in your horse trailer for extended periods, as the temperature can shift dramatically.&nbsp;</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Instead, select a cool, dry space with airflow. Ideal conditions include temperatures under 40°C and humidity below 70%. Further, never store your boots in sealed containers. These trap moisture and limit ventilation - two major risk factors for hydrolysis.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Keep the Velcro straps closed to avoid debris buildup. Additionally, store your boots “closed and fastened”, as you would when on your horse's hoof.&nbsp;</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Special Consideration: What Is Hydrolysis?</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"style":{"spacing":{"margin":{"top":"32px","bottom":"0"}}}} -->
<p style="margin-top:32px;margin-bottom:0">When storing your Cavallo Hoof Boots, it is essential to understand hydrolysis, a chemical reaction that can affect your boots over time. Firstly, Hydrolysis is the breakdown of bonds within materials like TPU (thermoplastic urethane), which is used to make Cavallo soles.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>However, This breakdown doesn’t happen overnight. It typically occurs after two or more years of storage in hot, humid, or poorly ventilated conditions. Temperatures exceeding 40°c, combined with humidity above 70%, significantly increase the risk.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Hydrolysis primarily affects boots that are stored and unused for extended periods, especially when they are kept in airtight containers or areas with limited airflow. Used regularly and stored correctly, your boots are highly unlikely to ever experience this issue.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>To avoid hydrolysis, store your boots in a cool, dry location with good ventilation. Never seal them in airtight boxes or plastic bags. Use them regularly or rotate their use if you own multiple pairs.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>If you're buying boots from a third-party seller, ask about their storage history. Poor storage conditions and age can compromise durability, even if the boots are new.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"style":{"spacing":{"margin":{"top":"32px","bottom":"0"}}}} -->
<p style="margin-top:32px;margin-bottom:0">Understanding hydrolysis gives you more control over caring for your gear. With the right habits, storing your Cavallo Hoof Boots safely ensures they stay strong, reliable, and trail-ready for years to come.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">What is the Durability and Lifespan of Cavallo Hoof Boots? </h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The lifespan of Cavallo Hoof Boots depends on various factors, including terrain, the speed and gaits of your horse, and how often the boots are used. These variables can all influence how well the boots hold up over time. For an average rider who uses the boots a few times a week, you can expect them to last around 2+ years.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Hoof Trimming</strong> - Keeping your horse's hooves properly trimmed and in good shape can help the boots fit better and last longer. Proper fit is essential for extending the lifespan of the boots.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Terrain</strong> - Riding on rough or rocky terrain can cause more wear and tear on the boots. Softer ground, like grass or dirt, is gentler on the boots and will allow for longer wear time.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><span style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px;"><strong>Speed and Gaits</strong>&nbsp;- Faster gaits, like trotting and cantering, can lead to quicker degradation due to the increased impact and stress on the boots.</span></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Frequency of Use</strong> - Regular use of the boots will naturally shorten their lifespan. Daily use wears the boots faster than occasional use.</p>

<p style="margin-top:32px;margin-bottom:0">Cavallo Hoof Boots Durability and Lifespan under standard conditions lasts an average of 2+ years. This includes use on various terrains with regular maintenance. Many riders, however, report their Cavallo Boots lasting 10+ years with proper care! Likewise, if you're using the boots 24/7 for rehabilitation, expect them to wear slightly faster.</p>

<p>Regular inspection and maintenance are essential to get the most out of your boots. After each use, clean your boots and store them in a dry, temperature-controlled area. Improper storage can cause the TPU in Cavallo Hoof Boots to degrade. Like a bike tire, your boots perform best when used regularly. Avoid leaving them in a horse trailer or exposing them to direct sunlight or freezing temperatures.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">When to Replace Your Cavallo Hoof Boots</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Even with the best care, Cavallo Hoof Boots will eventually show signs of wear. The most important area to inspect is the sole. Here’s what to look for:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Tread Condition</strong>: The tread provides traction and protection. Over time, it wears down, reducing effectiveness. If the tread is significantly worn, it’s time to replace the boots.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Holes in the Sole</strong>: Check the bottom for holes or deep wear spots. A hole in the sole compromises protection, which can lead to discomfort or injury for your horse.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>When your boots eventually wear through, it’s best to invest in a new pair to keep your horse protected. However, don’t discard your old boots! They can still be useful. Old Cavallo Hoof Boots can be repurposed. Use them as spare boots or repurpose them as medical boots to protect your horse during recovery from injuries or hoof conditions.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

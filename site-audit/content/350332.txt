<h3 class="wp-block-heading">How Hoof Forces Work</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading --></div>
<!-- /wp:group -->

<!-- wp:group {"style":{"spacing":{"padding":{"top":"var:preset|spacing|md","bottom":"var:preset|spacing|md"}}},"backgroundColor":"light-red-1","layout":{"type":"constrained"}} -->
<div class="wp-block-group has-light-red-1-background-color has-background" style="padding-top:var(--wp--preset--spacing--md);padding-bottom:var(--wp--preset--spacing--md)"><!-- wp:columns -->
<div class="wp-block-columns"><!-- wp:column -->
<div class="wp-block-column"><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>When a horse moves, each hoof experiences powerful forces as it contacts the ground. Understanding these forces helps researchers and veterinarians better understand equine locomotion.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Ground Reaction Force:</strong> The force generated when the hoof pushes against the ground during movement.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Weight Distribution:</strong> Healthy horses distribute weight evenly between limbs.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Loading Rate:</strong> How quickly force is applied to the hoof during impact.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Stride Dynamics: </strong>Stride length, timing, and limb coordination influence overall movement efficiency.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list --></div>
<!-- /wp:column -->

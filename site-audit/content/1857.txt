<span style="color: #000000;">There's a new member of our Cavallo Team and we can't wait to tell you about "The Donkey Farrier"!</span>

<span style="color: #000000;"><a style="color: #13aff0;" href="https://www.holistichooves.com/about" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><img class="wp-image-51009 alignright" src="http://cavallo.local/wp-content/uploads/2020/09/Megan-Hensley-Donkey-farrier.3-1-450x315.jpg" alt="Megan Hensley Donkey farrier trimmer Cavallo Horse Hoof Boots" width="431" height="431" /></a></span><span style="color: #000000;"><a style="color: #000000;" href="https://www.holistichooves.com/about" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Megan Hen</a></span><a style="color: #000000;" href="https://www.holistichooves.com/about" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">sle</a><span style="color: #000000;"><a style="color: #000000;" href="https://www.holistichooves.com/about" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">y is known as "The Donkey Farrier"</a>. Based in Fortuna, California, Megan offers in-person trimming sessions for donkeys and mules. She's also well-known for her online courses, sharing her expertise about donkeys’ and mules' unique hooves and specific health and trimming needs. Her knowledge and her approachable nature have made her a valued resource in the "long-eared" community.  She also helps animal owners select and fit Cavallo Boots!</span>

<span style="color: #000000;">I know that Cavallo has helped many donkeys and mules over the years with their hoof care needs. We know that their hooves are different from horses and we're excited to have such an expert on our team.</span>
<h3><span style="color: #000000;">What Drives Megan?</span></h3>
<span style="color: #000000;"> Megan has used Cavallos since 2011. I asked what it meant to her to be part of Cavallo, and here's her enthusiastic answer:  “I am absolutely thrilled to join the Cavallo team. It will help me to educate owners of donkeys and mules around the world through Cavallo's extensive platform. A lot of people who have donkeys don’t know how to train them and don’t have resources for how to work with them. Some farriers won’t even go, or they want them tied, or even on the ground or sedated to work with them. I have focused my training and education on all to do with hoof care for donkeys and mules—and teaching people to work with them kindly.”</span>

<span style="color: #000000;">What drove Megan to become the cutting-edge hoof care practitioner that she is today? We'll let <a style="color: #000000;" href="https://thesoulofahorse.com/blog/about-joe-kathleen/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">our friend, Joe Camp</a>, tell you in his own words.  Let us take you back to a guest blog he did for us many years ago when he became part of Megan's "AHA!" moment! <strong> <a href="http://cavallo.local/a-role-model-for-traditional-farriers/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">READ HERE!</a></strong></span>
<h3><span style="color: #000000;"><strong>A Fresh, Kinder Approach</strong></span></h3>
<span style="color: #000000;"><img class="alignleft wp-image-51012 size-large" src="http://cavallo.local/wp-content/uploads/2020/09/Megan-Hensley-Donkey-farrier8-2-1-300x300.jpg" alt="Megan Hensley Donkey farrier Cavallo Hoof Boots Trimmer" width="324" height="452" />Megan began working with donkeys at the start of her trimming career in 2006. After interning with a donkey-focused trimmer, she learned how to work with donkeys without a fight.</span>

<span style="color: #000000;">“<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Donkey" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Donkeys are a different species</a>,” Hensely explains. “In the wild, they don’t run like horses do. They turn and face predators. Donkeys won’t tolerate the traditional approaches.”</span>
<h3><span style="color: #000000;">What makes Hensley’s work different? </span></h3>
<span style="color: #000000;">“When I go to work with a donkey, I walk in to meet them without my farrier tools,” she says. “Later, with a halter on, I’ll play with them and pick up their feet. They may realize I’m a farrier, but they like me already. We may not even trim on the first visit—so that they will associate me with a good experience.”</span>

<span style="color: #000000;">In 2018, Megan deemed herself “The Donkey Farrier” and travelled the state of California to work with donkeys. “I drove all over, helping donkeys that the owners said no one else could or would trim.”</span>
<h3><span style="color: #000000;"><strong>Differing Donkey Hooves</strong></span></h3>
<span style="color: #000000;">What makes donkey hooves different? Megan says that donkeys have more soft tissue and that they have a steeper hoof angle. The donkey’s frog sits farther back and the heel bulbs stick out a bit farther than on a horse.</span>

<span style="color: #000000;">Megan is determined to take her specific teaching about donkeys and their hooves to a world-wide audience: </span><span style="color: #000000;">“I want to challenge the farrier industry to look at donkeys differently,” she says. “I’d like to see donkey farriers become a niche-just like exotic farriers who trim at the zoos or like some farriers are known specifically for helping laminitis.”</span>
<h3><span style="color: #000000;"><strong>Why Megan's Clients Love Cavallos</strong></span></h3>
<span style="color: #000000;"><img class="wp-image-51014 size-large alignright" src="http://cavallo.local/wp-content/uploads/2020/09/Megan-Hensley-Donkey-farrier9-1-450x315.jpg" alt="Megan Hensley Mule Donkey farrier Cavallo Hoof Boots" width="452" height="452" />Megan says she has seen donkeys and mules feel better instantly when wearing boots. “You know what I love about the Cavallo Hoof Boot?” she asks. “It’s the instant comfort that you can provide for the animal. If your equine has any pain and maybe you don’t even know why yet—you can put these boots on and get some instant pain relief. You can’t say that for metal shoes. Cavallos can make them feel better.”</span>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">What is Laminitis? </h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Laminitis is a painful hoof condition caused by inflammation of the laminae—the interlocking structures that support the coffin bone within the hoof. When inflamed, the laminae is weakened, compromising the coffin bone’s stability and potentially leading to rotation or sinking, which causes severe pain. For instance, imagine Velcro pulling apart—this separation within the hoof can be devastating!</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>In short, the most common cause of laminitis is insulin-induced laminitis, often linked to insulin resistance (IR). Horses with IR struggle to regulate glucose levels because their insulin-responsive cells do not react properly to insulin. This leads to excess insulin in the system, which can trigger laminitis.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>If the cause of laminitis isn’t immediately apparent—such as infection or toxic plant ingestion—treat it as an insulin-induced case. Most importantly, the immediate steps include switching your horse to a low-NSC (non-structural carbohydrate) diet with balanced vitamins and minerals. Meanwhile, always consult with your veterinarian to develop an effective treatment and rehabilitation plan.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Cavallo Hoof Boots for Laminitis provide extra cushioning and support, reducing pressure on sensitive hooves and aiding in comfort and recovery. Their shock-absorbing design helps stabilize the hoof, making movement more comfortable during rehabilitation.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Can Laminitis Be "Fixed"? </h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"style":{"spacing":{"margin":{"top":"32px","bottom":"0"}}}} -->
<p style="margin-top:32px;margin-bottom:0">There is no single cure for laminitis, but full rehabilitation is possible by eliminating risk factors and making necessary lifestyle changes. Some horses may need short-term anti-inflammatory medication, while others can recover through dietary and management adjustments alone.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>If your horse’s laminitis is caused by Pituitary Pars Intermedia Dysfunction (PPID, or Cushing’s Disease), ongoing medication like pergolide may be required. However, for many horses, removing high-sugar and high-starch feeds plays a critical role in recovery. Combined with a proper barefoot trim, this approach helps prevent future laminitis episodes.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"style":{"spacing":{"margin":{"top":"32px","bottom":"0"}}}} -->
<p style="margin-top:32px;margin-bottom:0">As a result, once the underlying cause is addressed, rehabilitation should focus on restoring blood flow and circulation to the hooves, as laminitis disrupts this process. Similarly, movement is essential for recovery, and Cavallo Hoof Boots for Laminitis provide comfort, encourage mobility, and support natural healing by reducing pressure on sensitive hooves.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">How Can Cavallo Hoof Boots Help Laminitis? </h2>

As your horse begins to stand, Cavallo Hoof Boots for Laminitis with padded insoles are essential for relief. If you're awaiting the arrival of the boots, a temporary pain-relief solution like taping foam to your horse's hooves can help.</p>

<p>Movement is critical for laminitis recovery, as it helps restore blood flow and circulation to the hooves. Once your horse stabilizes, hand-walking in Cavallo Hoof Boots will not only enhance comfort but also speed up the healing process. These boots allow your horse to move more freely, even when they would otherwise struggle.</p>

<p>By offering support and protection, Cavallo Hoof Boots for Laminitis encourage your horse to stay active and engage with their environment. With Cavallo Hoof Boots, you can trust that your horse's hooves will remain comfortable and protected throughout their healing journey.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Can You Use Cavallo Hoof Boots 24/7?</h2>

<p style="margin-top:32px;margin-bottom:0">Yes! Cavallo Hoof Boots support full-time use with their quick-drying construction and signature side drainage holes.

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">What Is Thrush in Horses?</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Thrush is a bacterial and fungal infection that affects horses’ hooves. It can cause dark discharge, deep cracks extending between the heel bulbs, and a strong odor. Many horses also experience hoof sensitivity and pain.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Thrush is often mistaken for a hygiene issue. In reality, it develops when the hoof environment allows harmful organisms to thrive. Most thrush-causing microbes are anaerobic. This means they prefer dark, low-oxygen conditions, such as wet or muddy footing. However, moisture alone does not cause thrush. Many horses live in wet climates without problems.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Thrush becomes more likely when moisture combines with poor hoof mechanics or other imbalances. These may include diet, hygiene, or environment. Poor hoof care can increase the risk, especially in horses with contracted heels, long toes, or underrun heels that reduce natural frog stimulation.&nbsp;</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The central sulcus and collateral grooves of the frog are prime targets for thrush. Becoming further vulnerable when frog function is compromised due to poor circulation, a weak digital cushion, and limited movement. Thrush should be seen not as neglect but as a sign of imbalance, indicating the hoof isn't functioning properly.&nbsp;</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Addressing thrush requires functional trimming, improved movement, and environmental management. Understanding the root cause is the first step toward resolution. When you focus on hoof function and circulation, treatment becomes more effective. A whole-hoof approach provides the best foundation for treating thrush with Cavallo Hoof Boots</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">How to Treat Thrush Effectively and Safely</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"style":{"spacing":{"margin":{"top":"32px","bottom":"0"}}}} -->
<p style="margin-top:32px;margin-bottom:0">Effective thrush treatment supports healing rather than destroying healthy tissue. While harsh chemicals kill bacteria, they can also damage living frog material. Products such as bleach, copper sulfate, or caustic dyes may offer short-term improvement. However, they interfere with the hoof’s natural healing process. Better results come from gentle, consistent treatments that protect tissue while addressing harmful bacteria.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>A functional trim is the foundation of thrush treatment. Regular, careful cleaning helps maintain these benefits.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Thrush treatment is most effective when maintained consistently. Common choices include daily diluted apple cider vinegar sprays, natural iodine solutions, essential oil blends, and hoof clays. Antibacterial hoof soaks can also support recovery.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Environmental management also matters. Reducing exposure to manure and standing mud helps prevent reinfection. Further, clean turnout areas and dry resting spaces support healing.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Diet also plays a major role. High sugar and starch intake weaken hoof horn quality. Poor mineral balance affects growth and immune response. Working with a qualified equine nutritionist can improve outcomes.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"style":{"spacing":{"margin":{"top":"32px","bottom":"0"}}}} -->
<p style="margin-top:32px;margin-bottom:0">Thrush rarely resolves with a single product. Long-term success comes from combining trimming, movement, nutrition, and gentle care. This whole-horse approach supports effective treatment of thrush with Cavallo Hoof Boots.</p>

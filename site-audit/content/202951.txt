<p class="has-text-align-center has-large-font-size" style="margin-top:var(--wp--preset--spacing--xl);margin-bottom:var(--wp--preset--spacing--xl)">Cleaning Cavallo Hoof Boots is quick and easy, thanks to their low-maintenance, high-performance design. Consequently, you can rinse Cavallo boots with a hose or clean them simply by riding through water; no special tools are required. This makes daily cleaning effortless for busy horse owners. When a more thorough cleaning is required, a mild antibacterial soap can effectively disinfect the boots without damaging the materials. Best of all, several Cavallo boot styles, including the Trek, ELB, CLB, and BFB, are machine washable at temperatures of up to 30°C. Simply toss them in your washing machine for a deeper refresh when needed. <h2 class="wp-block-heading">Everyday Cavallo Hoof Boot Cleaning Made Easy</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Cleaning Cavallo Hoof Boots doesn’t have to be complicated. In fact, most of the time, it’s as simple as riding through water! As a result, a quick splash in a puddle or creek can loosen and rinse away most trail debris. After your ride, a gentle spray from a hose will remove remaining mud and dirt.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Further, thanks to their sleek design, Cavallo boots don’t trap debris. Their smooth outer shell and open tread help keep buildup to a minimum. For example, just hold the boot under running water and flex it slightly to dislodge trapped stones or dried mud.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>As a result, you don’t need special cleaning agents to clean your Cavallo boots. For most riders, rinsing with fresh water is enough. This everyday method keeps your boots clean, functional, and looking sharp—without adding work to your grooming routine. Make it a quick habit after rides, and your boots will last longer and perform better.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">When to Deep Clean or Disinfect Your Cavallo Hoof Boots</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"style":{"spacing":{"margin":{"top":"32px","bottom":"0"}}}} -->
<p style="margin-top:32px;margin-bottom:0">Sometimes, your Cavallo Hoof Boots need more than a rinse. Perhaps your horse wore them due to a hoof infection or abscess. In such cases, deep cleaning is beneficial in eliminating any harmful bacteria.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>To disinfect Cavallo boots, use a mild antibacterial soap. Firstly, mix it with warm water, and apply using a sponge or soft brush. In addition, scrub gently, paying close attention to the sole and closure areas. Avoid harsh chemicals or high-heat drying, which can damage the boot materials.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Let the boots air-dry in a well-ventilated area, away from direct heat or sunlight. Never place them near a heater or on a radiator. This can cause warping or cracking.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"style":{"spacing":{"margin":{"top":"32px","bottom":"0"}}}} -->
<p style="margin-top:32px;margin-bottom:0">Disinfecting your boots helps prevent fungal and bacterial issues. It’s especially helpful for horses recovering from abscesses, thrush, or laminitis, who may be wearing the boots full time. Regular deep cleaning also reduces odours and maintains overall boot hygiene. With just a little care, cleaning Cavallo Hoof Boots keeps your horse safe and your gear in top shape.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Machine-Washing Cavallo Hoof Boots and Cleaning Velcro</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Did you know that some Cavallo boots are machine washable? It’s true! The <strong>Trek, ELB, CLB, and BFB</strong> styles can go right in your washing machine. This is a huge time-saver when your boots need a full refresh.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>To machine wash, first remove any major debris or stones from the sole. Then, place the boots in a laundry bag or pillowcase to protect your machine. Wash on a gentle cycle with cold or lukewarm water—no more than 30°C (86°F). Use a mild detergent or plain water.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Secondly, once the cycle is complete, let the boots air-dry completely. Do not put them in a dryer. As always, keep them out of direct heat or full sun.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>This washability makes cleaning Cavallo Hoof Boots even easier, especially after muddy rides or rehab turnout. You get a deep clean with zero scrubbing, and your boots come out looking nearly new. It’s simple, effective, and Cavallo-tough!</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Not to be forgotten, your Velcro closures can also be easily cleaned. Over time, your Velcro can collect dirt, hair, and debris, which may reduce its grip. To clean them, use a stiff brush or a fine-toothed metal comb. Gently rake through the hook-and-loop material to lift out buildup. Regular Velcro cleaning helps your boots stay secure and last longer! If your Velcro straps do wear out, you can easily purchase replacement straps.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>A Meeting of Minds</strong></h4>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Imagine 600 people grouped together who all love horses, and want to help themselves and others heal. PTSD, sexual abuse and autism are but some of the conditions that can be successfully healed by horses. <a href="https://www.pathintl.org/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Professional Association of Therapeutic Horsemanship (PATH)</a> does just that!</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Jenny Mundell, <a href="http://cavallo.local/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Cavallo</a> Customer Experience Guru, went on down to Texas to hold a place at the PATH conference as an honoured sponsor.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":4} -->
<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>PATH Conference and Awards Luncheon</strong></h4>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Between the moving talk by keynote speaker <a href="https://themighty.com/author/cara-khan/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Cara Elizabeth Khan</a> , and <a href="https://www.pathintl.org/path-intl-conferences/international/awards/1358-2017-path-intl-award-winners" target="_blank" rel="noopener">the nominations and awards received by PATH’s very deserving members</a> there was not a dry eye in the house!&nbsp; A touching and inspirational experience for all attendees, the joy, the hard work, the hardships and the rewards were highlighted amongst the tears.&nbsp; Beautifully produced videos about award winners and nominees conveyed the shared member experience. Bottom line - overcoming barriers in life through therapeutic riding.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Jenny was impressed by the devotion and commitment shown by the<a href="https://www.pathintl.org/about-path-intl/contact-us" target="_blank" rel="noopener"> staff members from PATH.</a>&nbsp; Present were PATH CEO Kathy Alm, Executive Assistant Kandis Branum, and Sara DePape, Rachel Moore and Jeff Kelling from the PATH Educational Department.&nbsp; And you can see our Dear Jenny in the photo, second from the left.&nbsp; If you haven’t had the pleasure of meeting Jenny yet, give Cavallo a call. To know this gal is to love her. <em>&nbsp;</em></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":4} -->

<p>Besides all the work and collaboration, there was some entertainment consisting of two active arenas with Instructors from PATH Centres giving demonstrations.&nbsp; Chris Cook from the <a href="http://www.wildhorsemanshipcenter.com/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Wild Horsemanship Center</a> in Florida was a pleasant surprise as an unscheduled guest speaker at the very end of the day. Chris and his very eager horse gave a heartfelt demonstration of how body energy can be used effectively when relating to a horse.</p>

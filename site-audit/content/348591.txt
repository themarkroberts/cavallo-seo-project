<div class="wp-block-column"><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Donkeys may be cousins to horses, but their hooves are no “mini-me” version of a horse’s foot!</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Built tougher, narrower, and with those signature high heels, donkey hooves are uniquely designed for rocky, rugged terrain. That means they deserve a boot that celebrates their special shape—not a hand-me-down horse-style hoof boot.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>After all… Donkeys aren’t just little horses—they’re total originals!</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Photo credit: Humboldt Ezelweide plans to try out our new LEB donkey boots on his darling duo, “When these blue CLBs are worn down…”</p>

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>His fabulous donkeys Capucine and Louane are living a great life full of walks in Belgium, “They are really comfortable in their blue shoes.”</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading has-text-align-center">Hoof Hug 101: Testing Your Fit from Heel to Toe:</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading --></div>
<!-- /wp:group -->

<!-- wp:group {"layout":{"type":"constrained"}} -->
<div class="wp-block-group"><!-- wp:columns -->
<div class="wp-block-columns"><!-- wp:column -->
<div class="wp-block-column"><!-- wp:image -->
<figure class="wp-block-image"><img src="http://cavallo.local/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/Perfect-fit-cross-section-of-hoof-in-Cavallo-Hoof-Boot-web.gif" alt="Example of a perfect Cavallo Hoof Boot fit" title="Example of a perfect Cavallo Hoof Boot fit"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image --></div>
<!-- /wp:column -->

<!-- wp:column -->
<div class="wp-block-column"><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>A proper Cavallo Boot fit means the hoof sits fully down into the sole, with the toe snugly in place and the heel bulbs comfortably cradled by the soft, padded back of the boot.&nbsp;</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The sole of the hoof should be fully supported, with no major gaps between the hoof and the sole/boot wall. While a wee bit of wiggle is good, if you can rotate the boot from side to side while fastened up, it’s likely too large. Give us a shout and we will take a second look at your equine’s size. ?</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Oooops – have to really push to get the boot in? That’s a sign they are too small. Remember, a boot that’s too large or too small simply won’t work. It’s all about the fit!</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Any questions about your fit? Reach out – we’re here for you!</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading has-text-align-center">Megan Brays from the Rooftops: LEB Review</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:columns -->
<div class="wp-block-columns"><!-- wp:column -->
<div class="wp-block-column"><!-- wp:image {"id":271456,"sizeSlug":"full","linkDestination":"none"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="http://cavallo.local/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/black_leb_donkey_hoof_boots_on_walter_-_megan_hensley_720.jpg" alt="Walter the Mammoth Donkey Tries Out the New Cavallo LEB Hoof Boot" class="wp-image-271456"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image --></div>
<!-- /wp:column -->

<!-- wp:column -->
<div class="wp-block-column"><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Megan Hensley, also known as The Donkey Farrier, is widely recognized for her fair, compassionate, and intuitive approach to donkey trimming. As a leading donkey expert, she has been involved from day one in the development of Cavallo’s new LEB (Long Ear Boots), designed specifically for donkeys.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Recently, Megan had the chance to fit a pair of LEB Hoof Boots on a client’s mammoth donkey named Walter. Here’s what she shared about the experience:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>“I’m very excited about the new LEBs – and had a wonderful time sharing them with a new mammoth donkey client. At our first meeting, I learned that he had gone out to the backcountry a month prior and was a bit uncomfortable in some spots. His owner was concerned as they wanted to return to the Trinity Alps this weekend.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>My eyes lit up, and I said I have just the thing! I’ve got a brand-new pair of LEBs that aren’t even on the market yet, and I just so happen to be looking for a mammoth to test drive them! Let’s see if they fit!And sure enough, they did!</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --></div>
<!-- /wp:column --></div>
<!-- /wp:columns -->

<!-- wp:group {"style":{"spacing":{"padding":{"top":"0"},"margin":{"top":"var:preset|spacing|xs"}}},"layout":{"type":"constrained"}} -->
<div class="wp-block-group" style="margin-top:var(--wp--preset--spacing--xs);padding-top:0"><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Walter went the full 16 miles in his new LEB boots. He had no real chance to break them in – only a few hours in his paddock the day before – but he still had no rubbing or discomfort from the boots. His owners kept wool socks on the whole time, and even left his boots on overnight. Not only do the wool socks help with potential rubbing (as there was no time to break the boots in), but you can also spray fly spray on the socks. He has two pairs – a short pair and a long pair that can be brought up over the knees to keep the flies off when out in the wilderness!</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The boots never twisted or turned, and they stayed in place perfectly. The back squished down to accommodate his heel bulb height, and I am absolutely thrilled! You can’t even tell that he was ever wearing boots- no sign of rubbing or discomfort.”</p>

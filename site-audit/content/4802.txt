<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Ever Find Yourself in a Scary Predicament?</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"fontSize":"normal"} -->
<p class="has-normal-font-size">While out on the trail, Amy George realized her compass was broken. She found herself in unfamiliar territory. Regrettably, when she paused to re-orient, her horse began sinking down into a bog. She had no idea how deep they would go and even wondered if they would get out at all. It could have been catastrophic.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Happily, as a team, they were able to get out safely and the most amazing thing was – Amy looked down to see that all of Smarty’s Cavallo Trek Boots were still firmly in place!</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"style":{"spacing":{"margin":{"top":"4px","bottom":"27px"}}},"fontSize":"large"} -->
<p class="has-large-font-size" style="margin-top:4px;margin-bottom:27px">Watch Amy &amp; Smarty During and After the Bog (Language Warning):</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Fortunately, Amy is a TREC GB (Great Britain) rider: a sport that tests the skills of a horse and rider in planning and executing long-distance riding in unfamiliar territory. It originated in France as a way of improving the skills of trail ride leaders and was introduced into the UK in the early 1990s. It is now gaining popularity throughout Europe.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Known as “the friendliest equestrian sport”, TREC is inclusive; designed to increase knowledge, awareness and enjoyment of the countryside for every rider, no matter their level of ability.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>TREC is a popular sport because you can have fun with friends AND your horse, regardless of skill (with the emphasis being on fun!). First-timers are always welcome!</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"style":{"spacing":{"margin":{"top":"4px","bottom":"27px"}}}} -->
<p style="margin-top:4px;margin-bottom:27px">Many TREC riders are using Cavallos for safety, comfort and protection during their rides!</p>

<h4 class="wp-block-heading">What is the Difference Between Cavallo Regular and Slim Soles?</h4>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"style":{"spacing":{"margin":{"bottom":"27px"}}}} -->
<p style="margin-bottom:27px">The Cavallo Regular sole is made length and width equal and is a good fit for a round hoof while the Cavallo Slim sole is ¼ inch narrower than long and a better fit for a more oval-shaped hoof.&nbsp;<a href="http://cavallo.local/how-to-measure/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Please visit our measuring and fitting</a>&nbsp;page for further information.</p>

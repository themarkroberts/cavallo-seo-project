<div class="wp-block-group"><!-- wp:heading {"textAlign":"center"} -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading has-text-align-center">Going Deeper with Equine Podiatry</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading --></div>
<!-- /wp:group -->

<!-- wp:group {"layout":{"type":"constrained"}} -->
<div class="wp-block-group"><!-- wp:columns -->
<div class="wp-block-columns"><!-- wp:column -->
<div class="wp-block-column"><!-- wp:image {"style":{"border":{"radius":{"topLeft":"10px","topRight":"10px","bottomLeft":"10px","bottomRight":"10px"}}}} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image has-custom-border"><img src="http://cavallo.local/wp-content/uploads/2024/10/Carole-Greg-at-Equine-Podiatry-Conference-UK-2024.jpg" alt="Carole Herder &amp; Greg Giles from Cavallo Hoof Boots at Equine Podiatry Conference UK 2024" style="border-top-left-radius:10px;border-top-right-radius:10px;border-bottom-left-radius:10px;border-bottom-right-radius:10px" title="Carole &amp; Greg at Equine Podiatry Conference UK 2024"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image --></div>
<!-- /wp:column -->

<!-- wp:column -->
<div class="wp-block-column"><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>As passionate hoof boot advocates, Greg and I felt right at home at the recent 2024 UK Equine Podiatry Association Conference. Equine professionals and horse owners alike enjoyed this conference, which hosted an impressive selection of speakers and experts in the area of equine health! </p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Launched in 2006, the <a href="https://www.epauk.org/about-equine-podiatry/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Equine Podiatry Association</a> (UK) exists as a self-regulating professional body for Equine Podiatrists practicing in the UK.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Equine Podiatrists are highly trained specialists in shoeless hoofcare, who work closely with owners to achieve the healthiest hooves possible for their horse.&nbsp;</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --></div>
<!-- /wp:column --></div>
<!-- /wp:columns --></div>
<!-- /wp:group -->

<!-- wp:group {"style":{"spacing":{"margin":{"top":"var:preset|spacing|xs"}}},"layout":{"type":"constrained"}} -->
<div class="wp-block-group" style="margin-top:var(--wp--preset--spacing--xs)"><!-- wp:heading {"textAlign":"center","level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading has-text-align-center">Why are Equine Podiatrists So Successful?</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The equine podiatry approach is somewhat different from traditional farriery, educating owners so they can understand how different factors influence the health of the hoof, helping to develop hoof-friendly horse management practices and providing close support and guidance as the horse’s hooves change.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The horse has the innate ability to heal itself, and EPs work hard to ensure that the maxim “do no harm” is followed. This hoof care system is based on a very effective model which takes a complex structure (the equine foot) and breaks it down into a number of key parts that enable the EP to assess the health of your horse's feet and recommend ways of improving that health. They never use any kind of shoe that permanently fixes to the hoof. </p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Members of the Equine Podiatry Association (UK) are required to hold a relevant qualification, and prove update their skills yearly through continuous professional development, ensuring high standards of professional competence are maintained.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --></div>

<h3 data-start="131" data-end="172">Cavallo Pastern Wraps – Sold in Pairs</h3>
<p data-start="174" data-end="567">Cavallo Pastern Wraps provide an additional layer of protection for your horse’s pasterns and heel bulbs when using Cavallo Hoof Boots. Made from soft yet durable neoprene, Cavallo Pastern Wraps help prevent rubbing, chafing, or irritation, especially during the boot break-in period. They can also be used to improve the fit of your horse’s boots or to “snug up” the fit after a fresh trim.</p>
<p data-start="569" data-end="1193">Pastern Wraps are ideal for horses with sensitive skin or those prone to rubbing during longer rides, turnout, or therapy. Designed to fit comfortably under all Cavallo Hoof Boot styles (sizes 0–6), these wraps are easy to apply and secure in place with strong Velcro fastenings. Wrap around the pastern and follow the boot fastening steps below for added comfort and protection. Cavallo Pastern Wraps help prevent small rocks, sand, and debris from entering the boots during arena rides or turnout. They are highly recommended for high-stress boot use, such as barrel racing, jumping, and other demanding activities.</p>

<h4>How To Use Cavallo Pastern Wraps</h4>
<p data-start="164" data-end="562"><img class=" wp-image-265062 alignleft" src="http://cavallo.local/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Untitled-design-1-300x300.jpg" alt="" width="180" height="180" /></p>
<p data-start="164" data-end="562">Step 1: While your horse is weight-bearing, wrap snugly around the pastern and fasten the main Velcro closure. The fit should be secure but not restrictive, allowing natural movement while providing full coverage.</p>
<p data-start="164" data-end="562"><img class=" wp-image-265063 alignleft" src="http://cavallo.local/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Untitled-design-2-300x300.jpg" alt="" width="180" height="180" /></p>
<p data-start="164" data-end="562">Step 2: Lift the hoof, and gently tug the wrap down under the heels, ensuring that about ¼–½ inch of the wrap sits under the heel’s weight-bearing points. This helps secure the wrap during movement.</p>
<p data-start="164" data-end="562"><img class=" wp-image-265064 alignleft" src="http://cavallo.local/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Untitled-design-3-300x300.jpg" alt="" width="180" height="180" /></p>
<p data-start="564" data-end="761">Step 3: With the hoof back on the ground, draw the elastic strap firmly upward at a 45-degree angle, like a sling supporting the heel bulbs. Fasten the elastic Velcro closure securely.</p>
<p data-start="564" data-end="761"><img class=" wp-image-265066 alignleft" src="http://cavallo.local/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Untitled-design-5-300x300.jpg" alt="" width="180" height="180" /></p>
<p data-start="564" data-end="761">Step 4: Place your horse’s Cavallo Hoof Boot over the pastern wrap and set the hoof back down. Fold down the small red Velcro tab to line up with the boot’s Velcro tongue.</p>
<p data-start="564" data-end="761"><img class=" wp-image-265065 alignleft" src="http://cavallo.local/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Untitled-design-4-300x300.jpg" alt="" width="180" height="180" /></p>
<p data-start="974" data-end="1275">Step 5:Close your boot as usual, and perform a final check of all closures to confirm everything is secure before riding, turnout, or therapy use. Enjoy!</p>

<h6><strong>Potential Hydrolysis effect on Cavallo soles</strong></h6>
Cavallo Hoof Boot soles are molded in industrial grade TPU (Thermoplastic Urethane). Under certain conditions, this compound may be vulnerable to a chemical reaction known as Hydrolysis. Hydrolysis is a bond breakdown which may occur at least 2 years after production. This happens only when boots are stored in unfavorable conditions and not being used. Hydrolysis may be avoided by ensuring good air ventilation and avoiding humidity over 70%. Keep the storage environment cool and dry with good air circulation. A combination of temperatures as high as 40 Degree Celsius with high moisture content, should be avoided.
Hoof boots that are frequently used in very wet conditions should be allowed to dry before storage and should not be stored in the original box or in areas of poor air circulation. Follow these instructions and your hoof boots will last free of Hydrolysis for many years.

<h3>X-Ray Results Everyone Wants to See</h3>
I shall try to keep this short, but when miracles happen it needs to be written. I have been in the horse industry since 1976. And I have witnessed many founders. And unfortunately none have ended well.  I myself had the devastating news that due to a thyroid problem - my horse went into a full founder. When I saw the x- rays (pictured above) I saw that only .5 mm were left before the coffin bone came through the sole. I set up a euthanasia appointment, as I just could not do it that same day. Then I hit the internet to try and find something that may help. I came across the <a href="http://cavallo.local/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Cavallo Hoof Boots web page.</a> I then called my local tack shop and they had the size I needed!  Off I flew to the store!  The horse was on heavy pain meds and anti-inflammatories and would not leave his stall, so when I got home and put the boots on, and then asked him to come out of his stall - HE WALKED OUT SOUND...my God, I fell to the floor and wept!

I iced his feet four times a day and he was able to be turned out in a small 20' x 20' pen to keep his body functions working properly and keep his state of mind positive. Founder involves so many aspects - but hoof support is huge. Except for the icing, he never had the boots off - and he would lift his feet to have me put them on.

"In January 2018, after a 25-year hiatus, I unexpectedly returned to the horse world. To keep things short and sweet, I was intrigued by how much things had changed.  Specifically, the holistic approach to horse care and going barefoot.  After months of researching the topic, I decided that it was what I needed to do for the health of my horses. The farrier I was using was not able to help me accomplish my goal but, luckily, I was referred to one who was. I was told it could be up to one year before I’d see positive results. The great news is, I could see the positive results almost instantly. Of course, there were naysayers around the barn who gave me all the reasons why I should keep shoes on my horses, but I’m not one to follow the crowd. After researching several hoof boot companies, I made my choice to go with Cavallo because, not only is the information they provide top notch, the quality of their boots is the same. I recently took my mare down to our local lake with her <a href="http://cavallo.local/product-category/hoof-boot/elb-hoof-boots/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Entry Level Cavallos.</a>   I was extremely happy with how well the boots performed.  Not only did they provide protection from harsh rocks on the trail, they stayed on in deep sand and down a hill to get there.  When I got her back to the trailer, I thought I’d have a big mess to clean up (especially with her excitedly pawing the water) but I was pleasantly impressed to find only a few small pebbles and a bit of sand. Thank you, Cavallo, for providing the solid information I needed to make the final decision going barefoot and for providing quality hoof boots to support that choice."

- Shelly and Fancy, Arizona, USA.

<h4 class="wp-block-heading">Is NOW Your Time?</h4>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>For many of us this year, the Summer lasted on into the Fall, and we're now just starting to feel the cooler weather rolling in.&nbsp; Unwelcome flashbacks of slush and snow are&nbsp;returning&nbsp;to mind, as we're slowly (and somewhat reluctantly!) accepting this time of change.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>But the change to Winter can bring great things!&nbsp; Barefoot things!&nbsp; Perhaps you’ve heard about the benefits of re-establishing proper hoof mechanism?&nbsp; Do you like the idea of allowing blood to flow freely into the live tissue of the structures that support your horse?&nbsp; And you know all about the debilitating conditions that so many horses are plagued with: navicular disease, laminitis, ring bone, side bone, splints, arthritis and chronic thrush...&nbsp; But did you know that these conditions can all be deterred by a practice that is simple, beneficial and cost effective?</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":4} -->
<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Have I got your attention?</strong></h4>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Now is the time to pull off those metal shoes!&nbsp; To allow the frog to make contact with the ground and pump fresh blood through the coriums of the hoof.&nbsp; To allow shock to be properly absorbed by the hoof, rather than referred back up the leg.&nbsp;<a href="https://www.thehorseshoof.com/Sshoeing.html" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>&nbsp;Studies have shown that horses endure an 800 Hz vibration</strong></a>&nbsp;each time metal shoe impacts a hard surface at a trot.&nbsp; Imagine how that feels after tens of thousands of steps?</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>You've likely heard that the&nbsp;cost of hoof boots (even with trimming costs included) amounts to less than regular shoeing.&nbsp; This financial difference is significant, but the real savings are the veterinary fees! Your horse’s overall strength, respiratory, circulatory and immune system will benefit.&nbsp; A properly functioning hoof has the effect of nourishing and revitalizing the whole horse.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

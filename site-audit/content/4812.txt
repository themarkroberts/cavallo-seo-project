<h2 class="wp-block-heading">It's Time for a Brand New Year!</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"style":{"spacing":{"margin":{"bottom":"27px"}}}} -->
<p style="margin-bottom:27px">To bring in the New Year, I am wishing you happiness, comfort and plenty of playtime with your horses. It’s been an ‘interesting’ year. Did you ever think we would really still be talking about Covid? I didn’t. As we courageously move forward, our values continue to shift. Many of us, including me, are searching for more meaning, purpose and understanding. I often find myself saying “I’d rather be happy than right” because I don’t know. I just don’t know. And I am OK with that. It’s liberating to admit I don’t know what the future will hold or even why things happen as they do. And rather than trying to make declarations, I am getting more curious to play, just to frolic in this field of all possibilities. I am becoming increasingly comfortable with uncertainty.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>It’s never too late to be playful. It’s never too late to have a happy childhood. And as Dr. Suess said, “You’ll never get bored when you try something new. There’s really no limit to what you can do.”</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Please know you are treasured. Thank you for being part of our Cavallo Community. Changing the world one horse at a time.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Yvonne Visits Shy Boy at Monty's Ranch!</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"fontSize":"large"} -->
<p class="has-large-font-size">Cavallo’s Yvonne Welz Knows How to Have a Great Holiday!</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"style":{"spacing":{"margin":{"top":"4px","bottom":"27px"}}}} -->
<p style="margin-top:4px;margin-bottom:27px">Have you ever commented or PM’d us through our <a href="https://www.facebook.com/CavalloHoofboots"><span style="text-decoration: underline;">Cavallo Facebook page</span></a>? Chances are, you’ve spoken with Yvonne! She took a vacation out west over the holidays, and she did it right!</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"style":{"spacing":{"margin":{"top":"4px","bottom":"27px"}}}} -->
<p style="margin-top:4px;margin-bottom:27px">The most memorable stop on her trip was Monty Roberts’s ranch, <span style="text-decoration: underline;"><a href="https://montyroberts.com/about-monty-roberts/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Flag is Up Farms</a></span>, in Solvang, California. This spectacular 75-acre farm is open for visitors, and it’s free to tour during their regular farm hours. The friendly visitor center staff said that there were 70 horses currently in residence, but typically there can be as many as 130. The farm has a full schedule of training events and courses that are open to watch. Yvonne was able to observe a private session with a trainer and young Thoroughbred, and she walked through endless paddocks and pastures filled with gorgeous equines of all different breeds.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">What a Year for Horses!</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"style":{"spacing":{"margin":{"top":"4px","bottom":"27px"}}}} -->
<p style="margin-top:4px;margin-bottom:27px">For many people and equines, 2021 was a year of ups and downs. Many areas had some beautiful Summer weather, but some areas became too dry and were ravaged by wildfires.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Trying to get horses and farm animals to safety is a challenging task, and many (the lucky ones) were relocated with short notice. These included elderly horses, horses with existing hoof issues, entire horse rescue organizations, and even horses whose feet were burned or injured from fleeing fires.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"style":{"spacing":{"margin":{"top":"4px","bottom":"27px"}}}} -->
<p style="margin-top:4px;margin-bottom:27px">These horses needed quick help and comfort so we swung into action here and packed up box after box of boots to send to affected horses throughout BC and the Pacific Northwest. Packing the boxes was the easy part – what was really tricky was finding the right people who could accept these shipments, and distribute the boots effectively to the horses in need. We ended up locating a few very special volunteers who were all working at the scenes of the fires. Read more about it all here:</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Emma Massingale's Train Video is a WINNER!</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"style":{"spacing":{"margin":{"top":"4px","bottom":"27px"}}}} -->
<p style="margin-top:4px;margin-bottom:27px">Emma Massingale’s charming film, <a href="https://youtu.be/Mn4-bLjw1KU" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener"><span style="text-decoration: underline;">All Aboard the Pony Express</span></a> has WON Best Children’s Short Film at the Equus Film Festival <a href="http://www.equusfilmfestival.net/about-us.html" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener"><span style="text-decoration: underline;">2021 EQUUS Film &amp; Arts Fest!</span></a> This short film shows Albert &amp; Ernie off for an exciting day out in Devon. Boarding a 1930’s Steam Train, they end up in the luggage compartment before disembarking at Buckfastleigh to go and explore some of Devon’s most stunning locations!</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><a href="https://www.southdevonrailway.co.uk/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener"><span style="text-decoration: underline;">The South Devon Railway</span></a> recreates the golden days of travel between the 1930s and 1960s. Lucky riders go on a trip back in time on this traditional Great Western Railway branch line along the River Dart, running from Buckfastleigh to Totnes Riverside. The railway links the popular attractions of the Totnes Rare Breeds Farm and the Buckfast Butterflies &amp; Dartmoor Otter Sanctuary, creating an action-packed and truly magical day out!</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"style":{"spacing":{"margin":{"top":"4px","bottom":"27px"}}}} -->
<p style="margin-top:4px;margin-bottom:27px"><em>(You may notice that Albert and Ernie chose sensible, yet fashionable footwear for their train trip! Those are CLB Blue mini boots, sparkling away in the sun!)</em></p>

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Carole Herder, President and founder of Cavallo Horse &amp; Rider Inc., is an internationally acclaimed figure in the equestrian community. Her books, There Are No Horseshoes in Heaven and Hoof Prints on the Journey, have become international bestsellers, further solidifying her status as a thought leader in the industry.&nbsp;Since 1993, Carole has been deeply involved in horse health. Cavallo is a global leader in developing, manufacturing, and distributing horse products. Carole’s passion for horse comfort is evident in her designs, including the innovative Cavallo Hoof Boots and Total Comfort System Saddle Pads.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>A true pioneer in the equestrian community, Carole is widely recognized for her expertise and contributions. She champions the benefits of keeping horses in their natural state, advocates for overall hoof health, and works towards the alleviation of debilitating disorders. Her achievements include being an honoured award recipient of the Royal Bank of Canada Woman Entrepreneur of the Year, British Columbia’s Businesswoman of The Year, and North America Enterprising Woman of The Year.&nbsp;<br>Carole is a certified Dr. Deepak Chopra Yoga, Meditation and Ayurvedic Lifestyle Instructor and a proud member of the Women Presidents Organization, supporting female entrepreneurs in every industry.</p>

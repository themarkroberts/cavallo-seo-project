<p>I came across two very different backs on horses recently. The first was an old gentleman who had lived a rough life being ridden three times a week for many years, had worn shoes all his life and probably an ill-fitting saddle too. He stood at 17hh (68cm) but did not look that large – his withers stood high above the rest of his body. Muscle atrophy had completely re-arranged his body posture and his spine protruded the whole way along his back.</p>

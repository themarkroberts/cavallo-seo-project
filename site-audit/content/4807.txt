<h2 class="wp-block-heading">How to Be Your Trimmer’s Favourite Client:</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"fontSize":"large"} -->
<p class="has-large-font-size">Great Tip from Customer Jennifer Wintjes:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"style":{"spacing":{"margin":{"top":"4px","bottom":"27px"}}}} -->
<p style="margin-top:4px;margin-bottom:27px">“<em>Did you know that adding carpet to my hoof boots, then some water, and horse for an hour makes trimming way easier? It’s awesome!!</em>”</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>It’s true! This is a huge gift you can offer your trimmer or farrier!</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Ground can suddenly become hard during extreme heat, and can wreak havoc on your horses’ hoofs. Hydrate those feet! Change your go-anywhere Cavallo Hoof Boots into a soaking solution by simply covering the drainage holes and filling them with water. Your Multi-Purpose Cavallos can ease your horse and make those hooves soft and buttery for your farrier to make quick work of!</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph {"style":{"spacing":{"margin":{"top":"4px","bottom":"27px"}}}} -->
<p style="margin-top:4px;margin-bottom:27px">They will love you! ❤️</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Tales of a Globe-Trotting Character Horse</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><em>“I met your Cavallo CEO, Greg Giles, and promised him I’d send some photos of my Arabian stallion, Grimm, in his Cavallos. As I scrolled through my old phone to find my best photos, I remembered so many other stories that Grimm has had in his 23 years so, far besides travelling across Canada and to Central America!</em></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><em>“Grimm survived cancer – he had a spindle cell sarcoma on his eyelid, and surgery to remove the mass followed by three rounds of chemo. He then had his left eyelid rebuilt with plastic surgery. Treatment was painful and long, and he was so sweet and understanding through the whole process.</em></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><em>“When I first moved him to Central America, the pasture I leased in preparation for his arrival suddenly sold… We had to put him somewhere, so he lived in our backyard at our rental house while we were building our own house! He learned to eat bananas and mangos from the trees in the garden, steal the grain out of the chicken feeders, hide in the garage when it rained, he even walked INTO the house a few times! He would often creep up behind me to hang out in the breeze of the fan when I was working at my computer on the front porch. He has made more than a few appearances in the background on my Zoom calls for work!&nbsp;&nbsp;</em></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->
